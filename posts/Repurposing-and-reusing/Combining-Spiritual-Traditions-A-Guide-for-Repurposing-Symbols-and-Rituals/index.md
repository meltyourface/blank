In order to address this knowledge challenge, we must first define and set the framework on three essential concepts: occultism, esotericism, and repurposing and reusing. Occultism refers to the study and practice of hidden, secret, or mysterious knowledge, primarily focused on spirituality or alternative religious methods. Esotericism, on the other hand, pertains to the exploration and use of inner, spiritual teachings that are accessible only to a select few individuals who possess the proper level of understanding or initiation. Repurposing and reusing involves taking established concepts, symbols, rituals, and practices and recycling them for new purposes or contexts while acknowledging their roots, original intents, and meanings.

To harmoniously combine items, symbols, and rituals from various spiritual traditions in occult or esoteric practices, we can follow these steps:

1. **Identification and selection**:
Begin by identifying and selecting the components from various spiritual traditions that you want to incorporate into your transformative experience. Study the history, underlying principles, and meanings of these components in their original contexts. This step aims to gain a profound understanding and respect for the traditions from which the items, symbols, and rituals originate.

2. **Analysis and contextualization**:
Determine the essential themes, teachings, or energies that resonate among the chosen items, symbols, and rituals. Recognize the commonalities and differences among them, and contextualize them within your own belief system, philosophy, or worldview. Think about how they can collectively contribute towards a greater, unified understanding of the interconnectedness of all belief systems.

3. **Integration and adaptation**:
Thoughtfully integrate the chosen components into a coherent framework that respects their original intent and integrity. You may need to adapt their usage, presentation, or interpretation where necessary, to ensure that each component works in harmony with others within the new setting. However, be cautious about altering any aspects that might compromise the core meaning or purpose of the components.

4. **Practice and evaluation**:
Put the combined items, symbols, and rituals into practice to create a transformative experience. Reflect on the effectiveness and the coherence of the combined components during and after the experience.
