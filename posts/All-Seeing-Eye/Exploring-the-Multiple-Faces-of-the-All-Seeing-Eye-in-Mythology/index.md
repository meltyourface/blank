  1. **Egyptian Mythology**: The Eye of Horus
  In ancient Egypt, the All-Seeing Eye was often associated with the god Horus, who represented the sky, war, and protection. The Eye of Horus, also known as Wadjet, was believed to protect the pharaoh and the Egyptian people by warding off evil spirits. The symbol is also associated with healing, as Egyptians believed that amulets of the Eye of Horus could heal ailments and restore wholeness to the wearer.
  
  2. **Christianity**: The Eye of Providence
  In Christianity, the All-Seeing Eye has been adapted as the Eye of Providence, a symbol representing God's omniscient watchfulness over humanity. The Eye of Providence typically appears within a triangle, which symbolizes the Holy Trinity (Father, Son, and Holy Spirit). This iconography is found in various Christian artforms, such as stained glass windows, and even on the back of the US one-dollar bill, where it serves as a reminder of divine guidance in the formation of a new nation.
  
  3. **Hinduism**: The Third Eye
  In Hinduism, the All-Seeing Eye is symbolically represented as the Third Eye, or the inner eye, of Lord Shiva. This eye signifies intuition, spiritual insight, and the connection to divine wisdom. Hindus believe that opening the Third Eye can lead to heightened spiritual awareness and the ability to perceive the true nature of reality.
