To synthesize the concepts of initiation, gnosis, and self-transcendence to create a transformative ritual that embodies symbolism from the ancient mysteries while contemplating the various repercussions on microcosmic and macrocosmic structures, we must first understand the significance of these key concepts and how they interrelate with each other and the wider esoteric context.

Initiation is a formal, structured rite of passage marking one's entry into a new phase or level of spiritual development. It involves educational, moral, and experiential processes that culminate in a powerful and often symbolic ritual, exposing the initiate to a deeper understanding of sacred and esoteric knowledge while challenging one's limitations and preparing them for more profound experiences. 

Gnosis is a direct, personal experience of divine knowledge or spiritual truths that transcends mere intellectual understanding. It is characterized by a deep sense of inner knowing and insight into the nature of reality, often accompanied by complex symbols, visions and states of consciousness. Gnosis is a transformative process, leading to a profound shift in one's personal and spiritual identity.

Self-transcendence refers to the capacity of an individual to surpass their perceived limitations and boundaries, awakening to a higher sense of purpose and connectedness to the universe. This can be achieved through various spiritual and psychological practices that facilitate the expansion of consciousness, ultimately leading to a greater sense of unity with the cosmic whole.

Now, in order to create a transformative ritual that embodies symbolism from ancient mysteries, we must incorporate elements and themes from diverse esoteric traditions. These could include, for example, the Eleusinian mysteries, the rites of Mithras, or the gnostic teachings of early Christianity. These rituals typically involve important symbolic representations and narratives aimed at facilitating initiation, gnosis, and self-transcendence.

To contemplate the various repercussions of such a ritual on the microcosmic and macrocosmic structures at play, we need to examine the intended effects on the individual (microcosm) and the larger collective (macrocosm). 

On the microcosmic level, the ritual would provoke a deep transformation within the initiate, fostering an awakening of inner potential, an enhanced spiritual connection, and a refined capacity for insight and understanding. It would also promote the shedding of old beliefs and limitations, leading to a more open, compassionate, and empathetic disposition.

On the macrocosmic level, the ritual would have a ripple effect on the collective consciousness, as each new initiate would share their newfound insights, wisdom and compassion with the wider community. This could potentially lead to a collective shift in paradigms and societal values, promoting greater harmony, spiritual growth, and understanding on a global scale.

In summary, harmonizing the concepts of initiation, gnosis, and self-transcendence in a transformative ritual that employs symbolism from ancient mysteries can have profound implications on both microcosmic and macrocosmic levels. It can initiate individuals into higher states of self-awareness, divine understanding, and interconnectedness with the universe, and contribute to the spiritual growth and evolution of the collective consciousness. By meticulously considering the integration of these concepts, one can develop a powerful, transformative ritual that evokes the potent energies and symbolism of the ancient mysteries.
