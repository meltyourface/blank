In elucidating the intertwined symbolism and practicality behind the Magnum Opus within the alchemical tradition, we must first understand its basic context. The Magnum Opus, or "Great Work," denotes the series of processes required to transmute base matter into the Philosopher's Stone - a legendary substance believed to possess the power to transmute base metals into gold, as well as bestow the gifts of spiritual enlightenment and physical rejuvenation. This profound metamorphosis encompasses both a spiritual and a material aspect, and is conceived of through the lens of the seven stages of transformation and the hermetic principles. 

To embark upon our journey of unraveling the symbolism and practicality behind the Magnum Opus, we shall first understand the allegorical significance of each of the seven stages of transformation.

1. **Calcination**: This initial stage symbolizes the breaking down of the ego and material attachments. In practical terms, it signifies the process of heating a metal to a high temperature, triggering a chemical reaction that reduces it to ashes.

2. **Dissolution**: Representing a deeper dismantling of the ego through introspection and emotional engagement, dissolution corresponds to the literal process of dissolving the ashes from calcination in a liquid solvent, such as water or alcohol.

3. **Separation**: The third stage involves the discarding of impurities in thoughts, emotions, and desire. In practical terms, this is achieved by separating the solute from the solvent, thereby isolating useful components.

4. **Conjunction**: Symbolically, this stage denotes the reunion of purified aspects of oneself, integrating them to create a heightened wholeness. Materially, conjunction manifests as the formation of a new substance by combining the purified components secured during separation.

5. **Fermentation**: Encapsulating spiritual rebirth and inspiration, fermentation translates materially to the introduction of a fermenting agent to catalyze further chemical reactions.

6. **Distillation**: Reflecting the pursuit of deepening purity on a spiritual level, distillation involves boiling the solution from fermentation and collecting the purified vapor to further refine the substance.

7. **Coagulation**: Culminating in the manifestation of the Philosopher's Stone, coagulation symbolizes the ultimate integration of spirit and matter, attained by condensing the purified essence and creating a solid, stable final form.

Now, we shall examine the connections between these stages and the hermetic principles, a series of philosophical precepts derived from The Kybalion, an esoteric text conveying the teachings of the legendary sage Hermes Trismegistus.

1. **Principle of Mentalism**: Reflecting the idea that "All is mind," the combined process of calcination, dissolution, and separation strips away false beliefs and base desires that cloud our perception of reality.

2. **Principle of Correspondence**: Reflecting the maxim "As above, so below; as below, so above," the stages of conjunction and fermentation embody the synergy between the macrocosm of the universe and the microcosm of individual experience.

3. **Principle of Vibration**: Emphasizing that everything exists in a state of constant motion, the processes of distillation and coagulation align with the idea that through intentional alchemical transmutations, we can bring our internal vibrations into harmony with the higher spiritual realms.

4. **Principle of Polarity**: Echoing the notion that opposites are simply gradations of the same essence, the Magnum Opus' seven stages exemplify the transmutation of one aspect of reality into another, achieved through confronting, reconciling, and transcending duality.

In conclusion, the symbolism and practicality of the Magnum Opus in alchemical tradition are brought to life through the interplay between the seven stages of transformation and the hermetic principles. Its philosophical cornerstone rests upon the idea that the physical and metaphysical realms are deeply interwoven, and that alchemical practices can lead to the expansion of spiritual and material wealth. By engaging with the Magnum Opus as a series of processes unfolding both internally and externally, initiates can experience profound growth and self-realization.
