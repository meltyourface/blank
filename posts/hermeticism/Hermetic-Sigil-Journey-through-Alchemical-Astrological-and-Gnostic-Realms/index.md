To design a sophisticated Hermetic sigil embodying alchemy, astrology, and Gnosis principles, we must first understand each of their core concepts and related symbols. Nigredo (blackening), Albedo (whitening), and Rubedo (reddening) are the three major stages an individual goes through on their journey to spiritual metamorphosis and evolution.

The sigil will be designed in three concentric circles representing these stages, with Nigredo on the outermost circle, Albedo in the middle circle, and Rubedo in the innermost circle.

Nigredo (outer circle): The first stage signifies the encounter with shadow aspects of oneself, purification, and working with dark or chaotic energies. The sigil should include the symbol of Saturn, which represents the liminal space between order and chaos, and Salt (a downward-pointing triangle with a horizontal line through it) from alchemical symbolism, signifying purification.

Albedo (middle circle): The second stage is the rebirth or refinement of the individual's spiritual self. Here, the circle is divided into six equal segments, each containing a planetary symbol, starting with the Sun (a circle with a dot in its center) at the top of the circle, followed clockwise by the Moon (crescent), Mercury (a crescent above a circle, with a cross below), Venus (a circle with a cross below), Mars (a circle with an arrow coming from it), and Jupiter (two crescents back to back, with a cross below). The central symbol will be the hexagram, which combines two triangles, one pointing upwards (Fire) and the other downwards (Water), reflecting the union of these elements and the balance of nature. Inside the hexagram, there is the alchemical symbol for Sulfur (an upright triangle with a cross below): the essential substance and energy that drives transformation. At the center of it all, place the symbol for Aether (a circle divided into eight segments), depicting the profound nature of enlightenment.

It is also essential to incorporate the four elements to represent the microcosmic and macrocosmic paths.

- Earth, visualized by a downward-pointing triangle with a horizontal line through it, can be placed at the bottom of the outer circle.
- Air, symbolized by an upward-pointing triangle with a horizontal line through it, can be positioned at the top of the outer circle.
- Fire, represented by an upward-pointing triangle, is already included in the Albedo and Rubedo stages.
- Water, depicted by a downward-pointing triangle, is also included in the Albedo and Rubedo stages.

This concludes the design of an intricate Hermetic sigil synthesized from geometric shapes and traditional symbology, symbolizing one's metamorphosis as a journey through alchemical, astrological, and Gnostic realms. It reflects the interconnectedness of macrocosm and microcosm, embodying the pursuit of inner transformation and enlightenment.
