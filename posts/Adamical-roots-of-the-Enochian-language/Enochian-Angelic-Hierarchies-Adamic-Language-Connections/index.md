To contextualize our examination of the relationship between the Enochian angelic hierarchies described by John Dee and Edward Kelley and the primordial attributes of the Adamic language, let's briefly discuss the origins and significance of both concepts in the esoteric tradition.

John Dee (1527-1608), a renowned mathematician, scholar, and advisor to Queen Elizabeth I, along with medium Edward Kelley, developed the Enochian system in the 16th century. The Enochian system claims to have established communication with angels through a highly complex cryptographic language expressed in the Enochian alphabet. The Enochian language is believed to be the original language spoken by angels, and thus has metaphysical and spiritual significance. Dee and Kelley delineated the angelic hierarchies into four Watchtowers, each associated with different elemental qualities (Earth, Air, Water, and Fire) and numerous angelic entities. The hierarchies represent various aspects of the metaphysical world and the forces that govern it.

On the other hand, the Adamic language, also known as the Language of Adam, refers to the original language spoken by humankind before the confusion of languages by God in the Tower of Babel incident, as recounted in the Bible. The Adamic language is deemed a sacred and divine language with primordial attributes, believed to hold the keys to profound understanding and knowledge of creation and the laws of the universe.

Now, let us elucidate the intrinsic principles and inherent resonances between the angelic hierarchies of the Enochian system and the primordial attributes of the Adamic language.

1. Divine Revelation: Both the Enochian angelic hierarchies and the Adamic language originate from a divine and supernatural source. The Enochian system was allegedly revealed to Dee and Kelley through angelic communications, while the Adamic language was believed to be bestowed by God upon humanity during its creation. Thus, both systems stem from a divine and esoteric source. 

2. Linguistic Morphology: The Enochian language and the Adamic language both possess an intricate and complex linguistic structure. The Enochian language consists of its own unique alphabet of 21 characters, as well as numerous tables, sigils, and correspondences. The Adamic language, similarly, is thought to have an intricate structure imbued with sacred and metaphysical qualities. The complexity of these languages emphasizes their esoteric and spiritual nature.

3. Cosmic Correspondences: The Enochian angelic hierarchies are organized according to cosmic and elemental principles, with each Watchtower associated with Earth, Air, Water, and Fire. Likewise, the Adamic language is believed to hold the key to understanding cosmic and elemental principles present throughout creation. The intrinsic resonances in representing cosmic forces between the two systems signify a shared understanding and appreciation for the interconnectedness of the cosmos.

4. Transformation and Evolution: The Adamic language underwent significant change following the Tower of Babel incident, evolving into a plethora of languages, while the Enochian language represents an attempt to regain that lost, divine knowledge. Both systems symbolize a transformation and evolution towards the understanding of sacred linguistic constructs in the esoteric tradition.

In conclusion, the Enochian angelic hierarchies described by Dee and Kelley and the primordial attributes of the Adamic language share an intrinsic connection through their divine origin, complex linguistic morphology, representation of cosmic correspondences, and joint symbolism of the transformation and evolution of sacred linguistic constructs. The exploration of these connections has provided initiates and students with deep insights into the nature of reality and the esoteric arts, as well as the role of language in understanding and connecting with the forces governing the universe.
