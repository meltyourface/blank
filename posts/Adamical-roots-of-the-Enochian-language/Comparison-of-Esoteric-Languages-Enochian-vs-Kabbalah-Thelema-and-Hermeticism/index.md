The Enochian language, also known as the language of angels, is an esoteric and occult language created by Dr. John Dee, a 16th-century mathematician, astronomer, and advisor to Queen Elizabeth I, and mystic Edward Kelley. During their work on alchemy, astrology, and spiritualism, Dee and Kelley claimed to communicate with angels through scrying, a form of divination using a reflective surface like a crystal ball or a mirror.

According to Dee and Kelley, the angels revealed the Enochian language – considered the original language spoken by Adam in the Garden of Eden – as a means of communication between humans and the divine. Dee claimed that the language contained powerful, supernatural abilities that could be used for both practical and esoteric purposes.

In comparison, other esoteric systems, such as the Kabbalah, Thelema, and Hermeticism, have also aimed to establish reliable methods for communicating with the divine. For instance, the Kabbalah, a mystical stream of Judaism, focuses on deciphering the divine knowledge embedded within the Hebrew language and the Torah. Its practitioners primarily utilize the Ten Sefirot, angelic hierarchies, and meditative practices to pursue intimacy with God and attain enlightenment. The Kabbalah also acknowledges angelic intermediaries but centers its practice more on the contemplation of divine attributes and understanding the mysteries of creation.

Similarly, Thelema, founded by Aleister Crowley, combines mystical elements from various traditions like Kabbalah, Eastern mysticism, and Hermeticism in its practice. Crowley claimed that divine entities, including the mysterious Aiwass, revealed sacred texts and instructions for practitioners to awaken their spiritual potential and achieve divine communion. This system, however, places a greater emphasis on the practitioner's self-discovery and empowerment, rather than relying entirely on angelic intermediaries for divine wisdom.

Hermeticism, rooted in the teachings ascribed to Hermes Trismegistus, a legendary figure blending the Egyptian god Thoth and the Greek god Hermes, compiles various spiritual beliefs, including alchemy, astrology, and theurgy. The Hermetic tradition focuses on humans' capacity to ascend and unite with the divine through personal transformation, spiritual practices, and knowledge of esoteric principles. Although it acknowledges the existence of angelic forces, Hermeticism accentuates the mastery and application of spiritual principles over the direct intervention of supernatural entities.
