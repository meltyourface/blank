To answer this challenge, we will dissect the symbolic significance of consummating the elemental within alchemical processes and personal transformation, delving into Hermetic and elemental correspondences, and finally drawing insights from the Tetragrammaton and the union of opposites.

Alchemical processes traditionally involve the transformation of base materials into something higher and purer, often symbolized by gold. These processes, while symbolizing physical transformation, have metaphysical implications as well. The consummation of elements (earth, air, fire, and water) within these processes can be interpreted symbolically as spiritual or psychological growth through the balancing and refining of fundamental aspects of the human condition. The Hermetic principle, "as above, so below," suggests a mirroring of processes at work in the universe and the individual soul, paralleling the outer and inner alchemical transformations.

Hermeticism, a philosophic, religious, and mystical tradition stemming from the 1st to 3rd centuries AD, includes the principles of alchemy. Hermetic principles are built on the seven Hermetic laws, which guide the understanding of elemental correspondences within the alchemical processes. In Hermeticism, the four elements are associated with specific qualities: earth (solid, stable), air (movement, intellect), fire (energy, creativity), and water (emotion, intuition). These qualities represent basic principles that, when integrated, lead to personal transformation.

Elemental correspondences, in both alchemical and personal transformation, point towards a natural law governing the combination and balance of forces in the universe. The elements are not only related to the natural world; their symbolic significance reflects the intrinsic nature of the human psyche. Alchemists often speak of refining and unifying these elemental forces to create the "Philosopher's Stone," which is emblematic of individuation, enlightenment, and wholeness.

The Tetragrammaton (YHVH or YHWH), the four-letter Hebrew name for God, also plays a role in understanding the consummation of elements. Each of the letters corresponds to an element: Yod (fire), Heh (water), Vav (air), and Heh (earth). This sacred name is said to encompass the entire creative process. The first two letters, Yod (masculine, fire) and Heh (feminine, water), represent polar opposites that come together to form the following letters: the Vav (air) and Heh (earth). This demonstrates the interplay of opposing energies and their synthesis in the greater whole.

Lastly, the union of opposites is a core concept in alchemical transformation and personal growth. This idea, based on balancing and harmonizing seemingly opposing forces, is symbolized in alchemical texts with images of androgynous beings or the "coniunctio" (conjunction), the coming together of masculine and feminine principles. Hermetic alchemists would often associate this union of opposites with the process of creating the Philosopher's Stone.

In conclusion, the symbolic significance of consummating the elements within alchemical processes and personal transformation can be understood through examining Hermetic and Elemental correspondences as well as the Tetragrammaton. The consummation of elements entails a harmonization of fundamental forces and principles, leading to profound self-realization, psychic integration, and spiritual growth. A careful balance and synthesis of these elements foster the union of opposites, pointing towards the ultimate goal of alchemical and personal transformation: the wholeness and enlightenment embodied by the Philosopher's Stone.
