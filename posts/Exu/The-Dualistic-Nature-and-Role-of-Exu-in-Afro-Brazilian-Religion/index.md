Within Yoruba mythology, Exu (also known as Eshu, Èsù, or Ellegua) occupies an important role as the divine messenger and intermediary of the pantheon of Yoruba deities known as Orishas. In Yoruba tradition, Exu is the guardian of the crossroads and the protector of communication between the spiritual and mundane realms. Thus, it is a facilitator of communication between humans and deities.

Simultaneously, Exu can manifest as a trickster spirit, testing the devotees' commitment, knowledge, and moral compass.
