To respond to this knowledge challenge, we will first need to explore the foundational concepts in the question and then proceed by analyzing the occult symbols that reveal an intricate connection between the Hermetic principle "As above, so below" and the perpetual balance between duality forces, ultimately highlighting a path to spiritual awakening and synthesis. 

Foundational Concepts:
1. Hermetic Principle "As above, so below" - This principle, stemming from the ancient wisdom of the Emerald Tablet of Hermes Trismegistus, suggests that the macrocosm (the universe) and the microcosm (the individual) are reflections of one another. In essence, this means that one can gain a deeper understanding of the nature of reality by noticing the patterns that repeat within themselves and the world around them.
2. Duality forces - Duality refers to the concept of two opposing, yet complementary forces that exist within all aspects of life. Light and darkness, chaos and order, and good and evil are all examples of duality forces. From an esoteric standpoint, recognizing the balance and harmony between these forces is essential for spiritual progression.
3. Spiritual awakening and synthesis - A spiritual awakening is a process by which an individual becomes aware of their true nature and transcends the limitations of ego-based consciousness. Spiritual synthesis refers to the integration of the various aspects of one's being, resulting in a harmonious and fully aligned expression of one's spiritual nature.

Now let's examine two crucial occult symbols that, when contemplated together, reveal the intricate connection between these concepts:

1. The Caduceus - This symbol consists of a winged staff with two entwined serpents spiraling around it. It is often associated with the god Hermes (Greek) or Mercury (Roman) and represents the balancing of opposites. The winged staff signifies divine wisdom, while the two serpents represent duality forces, often interpreted as polarity energies like masculine-feminine, yin-yang, and light-darkness. By focusing on the Caduceus, one recognizes the importance of merging opposites to achieve harmony and a higher spiritual state.

2. The Hexagram or Seal of Solomon - Also known as the Star of David, the Hexagram is formed by the intersection of two equilateral triangles, one pointing upwards (symbolizing the spiritual realm or "above") and the other pointing downwards (representing the physical world or "below"). This symbol encapsulates the idea of "As above, so below," representing the connection between the macrocosm and microcosm. The union of the triangles also expresses the balance between the duality aspects of light and darkness, masculine and feminine, and spiritual and physical.

Key Themes, Ideas, and Conclusions:
- The Hermetic principle "As above, so below" conveys the interconnectedness between the macrocosm (universe) and microcosm (individual), as well as the repetitive patterns that permeate both levels.
- Duality forces, such as light and darkness, are vital concepts in understanding the esoteric aspects of existence and spiritual progression.
- The Caduceus and Hexagram, when contemplated together, reveal the intricate relationship between the Hermetic principle, duality forces, and the path to spiritual awakening and synthesis.
- By examining these symbols and understanding their implications, students and initiates can appreciate the wisdom of harmonizing opposites and begin to integrate their own polarities in pursuit of spiritual growth.
