In order to address this knowledge challenge, it's important to first provide an overview of each of the referenced sources, as well as the Tetragrammaton, to gain a comprehensive understanding of their shared principles.

1. The Seven Hermetic Principles in the Kybalion: This esoteric text outlines seven fundamental philosophical principles attributed to Hermes Trismegistus, a legendary Hellenistic figure believed to be the founder of Western alchemy and Hermeticism. These principles are Mentalism, Correspondence, Vibration, Polarity, Rhythm, Cause and Effect, and Gender.

2. Azoth: In alchemy, Azoth is considered the "universal solvent" or "universal medicine," a transformative substance that represents both the origin and completion of the alchemical process. It is the spiritual essence that is believed to pervade all matter and unites the physical and the ethereal.

3. The Emerald Tablet: This artifact is an iconic piece of Hermetic literature containing the well-known phrase "As above, so below," which implies that what occurs on the macrocosmic level is mirrored on the microcosmic level, and vice versa. The tablet is a fundamental source within the Western esoteric tradition.

4. The Tetragrammaton: Represented by the Hebrew characters YHVH (Yod Heh Vav Heh), the Tetragrammaton is the unpronounceable, ineffable name of God, often translated as "I am that I am" or "I will be what I will be." It holds great significance as an emblem of divine power and the origin of creation.

Now, let's identify the shared principles between these concepts and construct a magical formula that demonstrates their synthesis, involving the Tetragrammaton for added depth.

At their core, the seven Hermetic principles, the concept of Azoth, and the axiom "As above, so below" share an underlying theme of interconnectedness. They aim to reveal a deeper comprehension of the universe and the ongoing process of creation, transformation, and dissolution. The Tetragrammaton adds divine foundation to this shared concept.

A magical formula that embodies these themes can be designed as follows:

Formula: YHVH _A_O_T_H

Explanation:

- YHVH: The Tetragrammaton encapsulates the various aspects of divinity and the creative force behind all existence. It sets the foundation for this magical formula.
- _A_O_T_H: This string represents the process of Azoth as the transformative essence that unites physical matter with spiritual reality. The spaces between the letters signify how the transformative process always remains incomplete and inherently fluid, with room for further development.

By applying this formula, the practitioner invokes the divine force of creation and transformation embodied within the Tetragrammaton. Combined with an understanding and application of the Hermetic principles (as above, so below), this synthesis eloquently highlights the inseparable interconnectedness of all aspects of existence.

To conclude, the key themes present in this knowledge challenge are the interconnectedness and transformative nature of existence, as exemplified by the Hermetic principles, the concept of Azoth, and the axiom "As above, so below." By devising the magical formula YHVH _A_O_T_H, the synthesis of these three sources has been demonstrated, with the Tetragrammaton grounding the formula in the divine source of the Western esoteric tradition.
