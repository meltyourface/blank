1. Calcination: the initial burning of the substance to break it down into its constituent parts.
2. Dissolution: the merging of the separated elements in a solvent, often water or alcohol.
3. Separation: the isolation of the desired components from the mixture, leaving behind impurities.
4. Conjunction: the recombination of the purified elements, creating a new, more potent substance.
5. Fermentation: the transformation of the substance through bacterial action or chemical processes.
6. Distillation: the purification of the substance through heating and cooling, concentrating desired elements.
7. Coagulation: the final solidification or crystallization of the perfected substance, marking the end of the process.
