To design an original altar for the Gnostic Mass, we must first understand the essential aspects of the ritual and the key themes. The Gnostic Mass, also known as Liber XV, is a public ritual created by Aleister Crowley in 1913 as a central rite in his religious system, Thelema. It serves as an allegorical representation of the spiritual journey and brings together elements of various religious and magical systems to achieve gnosis.

In the creation of this original altar, let's incorporate five essential elements symbolizing the key aspects of the Gnostic Mass and connect them to the various stages in the ritual. After considering the design, I will summarize the essential themes and ideas present in the altar layout.

#### Element 1: A Double-Cubed Altar

At the central stage of the design is a double-cubed altar. The double cube represents the fusion of the macrocosm and microcosm, symbolizing the union of the divine and human worlds. The altar's design will incorporate the four classical elements, Fire, Water, Air, and Earth, represented by four different colors on each side of the double cube, which signifies the balance and stability of the forces in the universe. It will be placed in the East direction to signify the rising sun and the beginning of the spiritual journey during the Mass.

#### Element 2: The Stele of Revealing

Placed visibly on the double-cubed altar, the Stele of Revealing symbolizes the communication between the spiritual realm and the earthly one, emphasizing Crowley's reception of The Book of the Law in Cairo in 1904. This artifact represents the transmission of divine knowledge, an essential step in understanding the interplay of the cosmic principles and their manifestation during the Gnostic Mass.

#### Element 3: The Chalice and Paten

A chalice filled with wine and a paten holding a consecrated wafer (called the Cake of Light) will be placed on the altar. The chalice represents the feminine aspect (the Priestess), and the paten (a sacred plate) represents the masculine aspect (the Priest) within the Gnostic Mass. These ritual elements symbolize the sacramental aspect of the Mass, where the Priest and Priestess unite and partake in the consummation of divine presence during the ritual's consecration. This union is a representation of spiritual transformation.

#### Element 5: The Serpent and Egg

A coil of a serpent with an egg in its mouth will lay across the altar's surface, epitomizing the cyclical nature of life and the eternal return. The Ouroboros, the serpent consuming its tail, represents the notion of regeneration, rebirth, and the timeless interconnectedness of all existence. The egg symbolizes the potential for spiritual growth and the evolution of the individual through the Gnostic Mass experience.
