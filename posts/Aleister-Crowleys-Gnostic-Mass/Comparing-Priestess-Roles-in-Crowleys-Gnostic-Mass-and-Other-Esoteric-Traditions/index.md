The Gnostic Mass, also known as "Liber XV" or simply "The Mass," is the central ritual of the Ordo Templi Orientis (OTO) and the Ecclesia Gnostica Catholica (EGC), a magical organization founded by Crowley. It represents a ceremonial enactment of the mysteries of Thelema—Crowley's spiritual system—which emphasizes individual will, divine relationship, and the discovery of one's unique purpose.

The Priestess is a major figure in the Gnostic Mass, serving as the feminine counterpart to the Priest. Her function is to embody and channel the divine feminine force, also known as the goddess Babalon or Nuit, which activates and balances the energies of the ceremony. Much like the Priestess in the Gnostic Mass, the symbolism of the Shekhinah emphasizes the importance of balancing both masculine and feminine elements in order to achieve spiritual revelation and harmony.

In Tantric ritual, the feminine aspect of existence is considered crucial in achieving spiritual growth and enlightenment.
