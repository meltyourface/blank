To design an occult altar that synergistically incorporates the elemental correspondences, deity archetypes, and ritual tools while maintaining harmony and balance, it is essential to have a deep understanding of the various components and their symbiotic relationships. In this response, I will walk you through the essential aspects to consider and the reasoning behind these choices. 
**Step 1**: Determine the purpose and intention of the altar
The first step in designing an altar is to establish its purpose and intention. This foundation will guide you in selecting the appropriate elemental correspondences, deity archetypes, and ritual tools that resonate with your intention. Decide if the altar will be used for a specific practice (e.g., meditation, spell work, summoning spirits), to honor a specific deity, or as a general space for spiritual growth.
**Step 2**: Incorporate elemental correspondences
Once the purpose has been established, we can start incorporating the elemental correspondences. The four classical elements (earth, air, fire, and water) play a significant role in the altar's balance and energy. Each element carries its own unique energy and attributes:

1. Earth: stability, grounding, prosperity
2. Air: intellect, communication, clarity
3. Fire: transformation, passion, courage
4. Water: intuition, healing, emotions

To incorporate these elements, we can use elemental representations such as candles, crystals, bowls, tools, or symbols engraved on the altar's surface. One way to ensure harmony and balance among the elements is to place each representation in its corresponding cardinal direction according to traditional correspondences:

1. Earth: North
2. Air: East
3. Fire: South
4. Water: West
**Step 3**: Selection of deity archetypes
Incorporating deity archetypes into the altar is integral for connecting with specific energies, wisdom, and guidance. Select deities that correspond to your purpose, intention, and personal beliefs. Here's an example of deities associated with each element which could be used:

1. Earth: Gaia, Cernunnos, or Demeter 
2. Air: Hermes, Thoth, or Tara 
3. Fire: Brigid, Hephaestus, or Pele 
4. Water: Poseidon, Yemaya, or Saraswati 

Add deity representations, such as statues, images, or symbols, to your altar and make sure they are placed with respect and prominence.
**Step 4**: Incorporate ritual tools
Ritual tools are valuable implements for focusing and directing your intention during rituals and practices. Depending on your purpose, make sure you choose the right tools, which may include objects like an athame (ritual knife), chalice, wand, incense burners, blessing bowls, and pentacle. Place these tools in appropriate locations, corresponding with the elemental directions, to work harmoniously with your altar's energy.
**Step 5**: Consider aesthetics and balance
Lastly, consider the overall aesthetics, arrangement, and balance of the altar. Ensure that the design is visually pleasing, practical, and symbolic of your intention. When arranging the items, distribute the elements, deities, and tools evenly to create an overall equilibrium. Symmetry and proper alignment will enhance harmony and the dynamic flow of energies between all altar components.

In conclusion, designing an altar that synergistically incorporates elemental correspondences, deity archetypes, and ritual tools involves thoughtful consideration of purpose, intention, elemental balance, and selected deities. By aligning the altar with cardinal directions, establishing balance among the elements, and selecting deities and ritual tools that align with your goals, you create an altar capable of empowering and supporting your spiritual practice.
