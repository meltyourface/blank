To answer this knowledge challenge, we must first establish an understanding of the key elements involved: John Dee's angelic communications, his pursuits in mathematics, science, and astronomy, and his Enochian system. Examining the interplay between these elements will provide insights into how they influenced and enriched each other, shaping John Dee's perspective of the Universe and his role within it. 

John Dee (1527-1608) was an English polymath and an influential advisor to Queen Elizabeth I. Dee had an insatiable intellectual curiosity, with interests spanning across various disciplines, including natural philosophy, mathematics, astronomy, and esotericism. His vast repertoire of knowledge allowed him to navigate comfortably between the esoteric and mundane spheres, enabling him to synthesize disparate ideas and create a comprehensive, unique worldview.

His angelic communications, or "conversations with angels," began in the 1580s, most notably with the assistance of his scryer Edward Kelley. These sessions became known as the Enochian system, named after the biblical patriarch Enoch, to whom divine knowledge was supposedly transmitted. The Enochian system is an intricate method of communication, comprising a complex symbol system, an alphabet, and a unique language. Dee believed that mastering this system would grant him access to a deeper understanding of the Universe and the divine realms.

As a mathematician, Dee was a staunch advocate of using the principles of Euclidean geometry in different contexts. He believed that mathematics, and more specifically geometry, provided a universal language and key that unlocked deeper mysteries of existence. The act of applying mathematical principles and structures to different fields of study was not new to Dee, and his work in angelic communications provided the perfect opportunity to do so.

Dee's expertise in science and astronomy further influenced his Enochian system. In the pre-modern era, the lines between astronomy, astrology, and cosmology were often blurry, and the influence of celestial objects on the natural world was commonly accepted. Dee incorporated this knowledge into his system, mapping correspondences between the Enochian entities and planetary forces. Furthermore, Dee was aware of the Copernican model, placing the Sun at the center of the Universe, which challenged the traditional geocentric view. This scientific development undoubtedly affected his thought process on the angelic hierarchies and the cosmological significance of the Enochian system.

The synthesis of Dee's various interests produced a unique and multidimensional system that seamlessly blended scientific rigor, esoteric symbolism, and divine revelations. Dee firmly believed that the angelic communications and the complex symbolic framework they provided offered a key to unlocking the deepest mysteries of the Universe and access vital knowledge that could drive humanity forward in terms of intellectual and spiritual progress.

In summary, John Dee's expertise in mathematics, science, and astronomy provided a robust intellectual framework that guided and enriched his work with angelic communications and the Enochian system. This fusion of disciplines allowed him to elevate his system from mere esoteric curiosity to a comprehensive and harmonious representation of the Universe's divine order. Consequently, Dee's understanding of the Universe and his role within it was profoundly shaped by the interplay between these diverse influences, extending his reach from the terrestrial sphere to celestial realms, ultimately uniting the human with the divine.
