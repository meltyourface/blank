Origin and Interpretation of "As above, so below"

The phrase "As above, so below" is attributed to the Emerald Tablet, a foundational text in the Western hermetic tradition. This ancient work, likely authored between the 6th and 8th centuries, is shrouded in mystery. "As above, so below" is often interpreted as a statement of the deep-seated connection between different planes of existence or realms of reality. It implies that actions, events, or patterns occurring in one dimension have parallels and consequences in others.

Image-making in Craftsmanship and the Hermetic Concept

Image-making in craftsmanship represents the physical manifestation of ideas, thoughts, and emotions of an artist through various visual mediums like painting, sculpture, photography, and film. The Hermetic concept of "As above, so below" in this context suggests a deep relationship between the tangible artwork (below) and the intangible, esoteric planes (above).

The Artist's Inner World and Spiritual Influence

The artist's inner world comprises their thoughts, emotions, experiences, and creative visions. These form the foundational building blocks of any image produced by an artist and are heavily influenced by various sources of spiritual inspiration. This spiritual influence within esotericism can come in multiple forms, ranging from divine or celestial intervention to mystical experiences and practices, all aimed at facilitating a connection between the artist's consciousness and the metaphysical realms.

In the context of "As above, so below," the artist's inner world (below) is a reflection of the spiritual influence (above) they have accessed through their creative process. It is through the image-making in craftsmanship that the artist represents the cosmic forces shaping their existence, internalizing and embodying the hermetic principle.

Artistic Medium as a Bridge

The chosen artistic medium, be it painting, sculpture, or any other visual form, serves as the link between the artist's inner world and the spiritual influences from esoteric realms. The artistic medium enables the creator to externalize and materialize the abstract aspects of their inner world, thereby making the invisible visible and connecting the microcosm to the macrocosm.

Key Themes and Conclusions

The Hermetic concept of "As above, so below" is closely related to image-making in craftsmanship through the intricate connections between an artist's inner world, the chosen artistic medium, and the spiritual influence from esoteric planes. By internalizing and embodying the idea that what exists at one level has parallels at others, artists are capable of expressing and manifesting complex metaphysical ideas through their works.

The artist's inner world and spiritual inspiration create a timeless, cosmic connection that is materialized through the chosen artistic medium, serving as a bridge between the tangible and intangible.
