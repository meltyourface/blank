In addressing this knowledge challenge, let us first develop a foundational understanding of the Left-Hand Path and antinomian rituals, and then examine the connection between taboo-breaking, individualism, spiritual evolution, and self-determination. We will also discuss the psychological effects and personal transformation that result from such practices and analyze the potential implications of these practices on achieving true self-awareness.

The Left-Hand Path is a term that refers to a wide variety of occult practices and belief systems that emphasize individualism, self-determination, and self-transformation, often through the exploration of darker or taboo aspects of human existence as a means of spiritual evolution. Antinomian rituals, in this context, are practices that intentionally go against established norms, moral codes, and societal taboos, which subvert dogmatic rules and allow the practitioner to seek personal enlightenment and power.

When a practitioner of the Left-Hand Path engages in antinomian rituals that involve breaking societal taboos, several things are simultaneously occurring within their psyche and perception of the world. Through deliberate rejection of societal constraints and expectations, they are freeing themselves from the limitations that external authorities or moral norms may impose on their thoughts, actions, and beliefs. This in turn contributes to a deeper understanding of individualism, as they learn to focus on their subjective experiences and personal perspectives, rather than adhering to collective norms or prescribed morality.

This act of breaking taboos within antinomian rituals can also contribute to personal spiritual evolution. By confronting and embracing the "shadow side" of both themselves and the world around them, practitioners can begin to integrate these seemingly darker aspects of existence into their personal understanding of reality. Through this integration, they can achieve a more holistic understanding of themselves and their place in the universe, ultimately becoming more complete and evolved beings.

Self-determination too is significantly affected by antinomian rituals. By pushing the boundaries of acceptable or permissible action and experiencing the consequences, both positive and negative, of exercising full personal autonomy, practitioners develop a stronger sense of their own agency and willpower. This agency can be a catalyst for personal transformation, propelling them to make conscious choices and direct their own spiritual path.

The psychological effects of engaging in taboo-breaking antinomian rituals can be profound and transformative. Practitioners may experience a sense of liberation and freedom from societal constraints and may develop greater self-awareness and an expanded understanding of the complexities of human nature. Self-insight can also deepen, allowing them to recognize and embrace their desires, fears, and motivations more fully, ultimately leading to personal growth and psychological transformation.

However, it is essential to acknowledge that antinomian ritual practices can potentially go beyond mere indulgence or transgression. Pursued responsibly and with intention, these rituals can open the door to deep self-discovery and personal evolution, as one seeks to understand and integrate all aspects of their being. It is necessary for practitioners to remain mindful of their intentions, continually reflecting on their motives and recognizing how their actions contribute to the greater spiritual journey they are embarking on.

In conclusion, the connection between breaking societal taboos within antinomian rituals and shaping one's personal understanding of individualism, spiritual evolution, and self-determination is multifaceted. These rituals provide a space for individuals to explore personal liberation, self-discovery, and integration of 'shadow' aspects of their being. The psychological effects can lead to profound personal transformation, opening up deeper self-awareness and insight. However, it is crucial to approach these practices responsibly and deliberately in order to achieve lasting and meaningful progress on the path to true self-awareness.
