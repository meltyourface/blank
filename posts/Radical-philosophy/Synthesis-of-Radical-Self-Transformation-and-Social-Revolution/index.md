In order to understand how the concepts of radical self-transformation in occultism and the pursuit for revolutionary changes in society under radical philosophy can be synthesized, we must first delve into what these concepts entail and how they have been developed historically. By doing so, we will be better equipped to construct a balanced understanding of personal and collective evolution, informed by the ideas and insights of both historical and contemporary thinkers from various traditions.

Radical self-transformation in occultism refers to the process of deep, spiritual change that an individual undergoes in order to transcend their current state of being, reach higher levels of consciousness, and tap into hidden powers or esoteric knowledge. This transformative process often involves engaging in practices such as meditation, ritual work, and the study of arcane texts and symbols. Throughout history, there have been many examples of individuals who have undergone this process, such as the ancient Egyptian and Greek mystery schools, the Hermetic tradition, various mystical sects within Christianity and Islam, as well as modern occultists like Aleister Crowley and Anton LaVey.

On the other hand, the pursuit of revolutionary changes in society under radical philosophy emphasizes the importance of political, social, and systemic transformation in order to create a more just, equitable, and liberated world. Prominent examples of radical philosophies include Marxism, anarchism, and various strands of critical theory and postcolonial thought that seek to challenge and dismantle oppressive structures and dominant ideologies. Esteemed thinkers in this tradition include the likes of Karl Marx, Emma Goldman, Michel Foucault and bell hooks, among countless others.

To synthesize these two seemingly disparate concepts, we must consider the ways in which both personal and societal transformation are closely interconnected. From an occult perspective, the radical self-transformation empowers the individual to better understand themselves and their place within the broader cosmos, providing them with the tools to confront and overcome personal limitations and internalized systems of oppression. This self-awareness and inner resilience can then be harnessed to affect meaningful change in the world around them.

On the flip side, radical philosophers understand that a truly revolutionary change in society is not possible without a corresponding shift in the attitudes, values, and consciousness of its members. As Brazilian philosopher Paulo Freire put it in his seminal work, "Pedagogy of the Oppressed," a critically conscious individual who recognizes and challenges the oppressive systems they live in is a vital catalyst for social transformation. In other words, both personal and collective evolution are necessary and mutually reinforcing processes.

In synthesizing these seemingly disparate ideas, we can arrive at a balanced, holistic understanding of personal and collective evolution, which considers both our inner, spiritual development and our engagement with the material, social and political dimensions of our world. This harmony of approaches is consistent with the ideals expressed by contemporary thinkers such as Starhawk, an influential writer and activist within the modern Pagan movement, who emphasizes the inseparability of personal transformation and collective liberation.

In conclusion, the synthesis of radical self-transformation in occultism and the pursuit of revolutionary change in society under radical philosophy involves recognizing the interconnected nature of personal and collective evolution. By understanding the inner journey towards self-discovery and empowerment as being fundamentally linked to our ability to enact meaningful change upon the world, we can develop a much more comprehensive and actionable understanding of how to bring about spiritual and material progress in our lives and the broader society. Key themes and ideas include the inseparability of personal and societal transformation; the importance of challenging and dismantling oppressive systems, both internally and externally; and the mutually reinforcing nature of spiritual growth and political activism.
