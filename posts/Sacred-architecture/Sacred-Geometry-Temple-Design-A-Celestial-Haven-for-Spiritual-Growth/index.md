The central space is devoted to the primary altar, where ritual ceremonies and initiations would occur. The central altar is constructed in the shape of a hexagonal star, which is highly significant in sacred geometry. The pattern resembles the Flower of Life and Metatron's Cube, both of which are key components of esoteric systems. Above the central altar, a skyward-facing narrow and tall pyramidal roof focuses the energies upwards and connects the temple to celestial bodies and the cosmos.

They are interconnected and carefully designed to channel energy from the outer periphery to the central heart of the temple. Each of these circles links to one of the eight points of Metatron's Cube, symbolizing the unity of heaven and earth.

At the entrance of the temple, a large Tree of Life mural adorns the floor, marking the point of transition between the world outside and the sacred space within. This symbolizes the interconnectedness of all things and serves as a reminder to practitioners of the spiritual journey they undertake when entering the temple.

The temple is deliberately orientated towards specific cardinal points which are based on key astronomical events. These alignments allow natural light to cascade into the temple through strategically placed openings during the equinoxes and solstices, recreating a celestial connection akin to that experienced at Stonehenge and the Great Pyramid of Giza.

The temple would be constructed with energy-conducting materials such as granite, quartz, and basalt, naturally enhancing the potency of the ritualistic space.
