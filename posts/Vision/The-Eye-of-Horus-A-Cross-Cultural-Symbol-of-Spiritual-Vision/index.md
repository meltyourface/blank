In ancient Egyptian mythology, Horus was the sky god, depicted as a falcon or a man with a falcon's head. One of the most recognizable symbols associated with him is the Eye of Horus, which represents both the left and right eyes of the divine. The left eye represents the Moon while the right eye symbolizes the Sun. Aside from the celestial connections, the Eye of Horus symbolizes spiritual vision and is associated with the brow or third eye chakra, which is considered the seat of intuition and inner guidance in various spiritual traditions.

The Eye of Horus also serves as a symbol of divine protection and healing, stemming from an ancient myth in which the god Seth tore out the eye of Horus during a battle to avenge his father, Osiris. In the aftermath, the goddess Hathor restored the injured eye, imbuing it with healing properties. In Egyptian art, the eye is often represented with an elaborately decorated wedjat, a protective amulet associated with royal power, divine healing, and rulership. This emphasizes its connection to inner wisdom and the ability to perceive and understand the mysteries of the universe.
