The nine realms of Norse cosmology, which are the interconnected worlds in the mythological cosmos, are as follows:

1. Asgard (home of the Aesir gods)
2. Vanaheim (home of the Vanir gods)
3. Alfheim (home of the light elves)
4. Midgard (home of humans)
5. Jotunheim (home of giants)
6. Svartalfheim (home of the dark elves and dwarfs)
7. Niflheim (a world of ice and cold)
8. Helheim (the realm of the dead)
9. Muspelheim (a world of fire and heat)

These realms are connected through the concept of Absence, referring to the balance and interplay between the manifest and the unmanifest, the tangible and the intangible, or the positive and the negative forces that permeate all aspects of life. Each rune carries a unique metaphysical resonance, allowing the precise manipulation of energies aligned with the associated realm, archetype, or force. Bindrunes, on the other hand, combine two or more runes into a single representation, generating a potent symbol with an amalgam of meanings, powers, and associations.

2. Understanding Absence empowers the practitioner to tap into the transformative potential of the hidden forces that lies dormant, generating change, growth, and metamorphosis.

3. The integration of various energies from different realms leads to a greater understanding of the interconnectedness of all things, emphasizing the importance of balance in both sorcery and daily life.
