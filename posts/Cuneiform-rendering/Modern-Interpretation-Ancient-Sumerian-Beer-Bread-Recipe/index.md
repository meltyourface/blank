Ancient Sumerian Inspired Beer Bread

Ingredients:
- 3 cups (720ml) lukewarm water
- 2 tablespoons active dry yeast
- 1/4 cup (60ml) honey
- 1/4 cup (60ml) vegetable oil
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 7 cups (840g) bread flour
- 1 cup (120g) barley flour
- 1 cup (237ml) ancient Sumerian-style beer (substitute with a dark, malty beer)

Instructions:

1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the lukewarm water, active dry yeast, and honey. Allow the mixture to sit for 5-10 minutes, or until the yeast has become foamy and bubbly.

2. Add the vegetable oil and salt to the yeast mixture, stirring to combine.

3. In a separate bowl, combine the bread flour and barley flour.

4. Gradually add the flour mixture to the yeast mixture, stirring until a sticky dough forms. If you have a stand mixer, you may use a dough hook attachment for this step.

5. On a lightly floured surface, knead the dough for 8-10 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic. Note that you may need to add more flour during the kneading process to prevent the dough from sticking to your hands and the surface.

6. Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover it with a clean cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours or until it has doubled in size.

7. When the dough has risen, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and prepare a baking sheet with parchment paper.

8. Punch down the dough and shape it into a large round loaf or two smaller loaves, placing them on the prepared baking sheet.

9. Score the surface of the dough with a sharp knife, making a few shallow diagonal cuts.

10. Bake the bread in a preheated oven for 30-40 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the loaf sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.

11. Remove the bread from the oven and optionally brush the top with melted butter. Then let it cool on a wire rack.

12. Once the bread is cooled, slice and serve with a side of ancient Sumerian-style beer (or other dark, malty beer) to pay homage to the original recipe and the goddess Ninkasi.
