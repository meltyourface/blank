To address this knowledge challenge, we need to first understand the key concepts and terms involved, including microcosm and macrocosm, correspondence, sympathetic magic, and astral and elemental forces. 

Microcosm refers to the smaller, human-centered world. It encompasses the aspects and elements that make an individual human being, such as the body, emotions, mind, and spirit. Macrocosm, on the other hand, is the larger world outside the individual, containing all the universal forces, cosmic principles, and natural phenomena. The microcosm operates at the individual human level, while the macrocosm operates on a grand scale, including the Earth, solar system, and universe.

The relationship between microcosm and macrocosm is based on the principle of correspondence, a concept popularized in the Hermetic axiom: "As above, so below; as below, so above." This principle states that what occurs in the macrocosm is mirrored in the microcosm and vice versa. In both worlds, all phenomena correspond to each other, and nothing exists in isolation. This idea is found in various esoteric and occult traditions, and it emphasizes that an understanding of one-world aids in the understanding of the other.

Sympathetic magic, also known as imitative magic, is a type of magical practice that involves manipulating the macrocosm (the larger world) to affect changes in the microcosm (the individual's life). In this magical context, practitioners use the principles of correspondence and similarity to establish connections between objects, symbols, and actions to achieve specific goals or states of being.

Astral and elemental forces are essential components of the esoteric and occult understanding of the universe. Astral forces refer to the energy patterns or structures created by the movements and positioning of celestial bodies, which can influence individual and collective consciousness, as well as physical reality itself. Elemental forces, on the other hand, represent the fundamental principles and building blocks of nature (i.e., Earth, Air, Fire, and Water).

Now that we have a grasp on the concepts involved, let's explore the practical application of these principles in practitioners' workings.

In esoteric and occult practice, an understanding of the relationship between the microcosm and macrocosm is crucial for creating an intentional and effective change. By recognizing that individual lives and experiences are interconnected with larger cosmic forces, practitioners can enhance their magical workings, such as rituals, spells, or meditation. For example, by aligning one's intention with the appropriate phase of the Moon or planet, a practitioner can harness the power of the celestial energies to manifest the desired outcome.

In addition, invoking specific elemental forces can also strengthen and focus practitioners' workings. For instance, if a practitioner wishes to bring about significant change in their life, they might use fire as an elemental symbol, as fire represents transformation and renewal. Alternatively, if they aim to bring emotional healing to themselves or others, they might channel the healing properties of water in their rituals or spells.

Sympathetic magic, in the form of rituals and symbolic representations, also allows practitioners to establish connections between the microcosm and macrocosm. By working with objects, symbols, or actions that resonate with their intentions, practitioners can tap into the macrocosmic energies and create a sympathetic link between the two worlds, thereby affecting change on a personal or collective level.

In summary, the relationship between the microcosm and macrocosm in esoteric and occult traditions is based on the principle of correspondence, as well as the interconnectedness between different levels of reality. Through sympathetic magic and an understanding of astral and elemental forces, practitioners can harness these connections to manifest change in their lives and the world around them. By recognizing the interconnected nature of existence, they can enhance their workings and make meaningful changes that align with both their personal aims and the greater cosmic order.
