Similarly, in Zoroastrianism, there's the cosmic struggle of dualism, where Ahura Mazda (the light) and Angra Mainyu (the darkness) battle for supremacy over human souls.

The Concept of Individuation:
Individuation, as introduced by the psychologist Carl Gustav Jung, pertains to the process of becoming a fully integrated and individuated person, with a balanced and harmonious inner life. In this context, the Legion of Night can be seen as an organization committed to fostering self-discovery and personal growth. They utilize esoteric knowledge, ancient wisdom, and initiation rituals as means to develop the individual adept's mind and spirit.

Through the study of occult sciences like Alchemy, Astrology, Qabalah, and Tarot, members of the Legion of Night are guided through their personal transformation process, where the symbols and archetypes associated with these esoteric disciplines assist with the integration of the conscious and unconscious parts of the psyche.

By embracing and acknowledging our shadow selves, an adept can progress towards the light – discovering deeper self-awareness, personal growth, and greater spiritual enlightenment.

**Conclusion**:
The Legion of Night transcends its symbolic representations within various cultural traditions by offering a profound examination of the individual's journey towards self-realization and spiritual growth.
