Alex Sanders: Life, Contributions, and the Alexandrian Tradition

Born in 1926, Alex grew up with an innate interest in witchcraft, with his passion for the esoteric fueling his pursuits throughout his life. It developed as an eclectic blend of ceremonial magic, Kabbalah, and traditional folk witchcraft, with further influences from the work of visionary occultists like Aleister Crowley and Eliphas Levi.

Key Tenets and Practices

The Alexandrian tradition is characterized by its dual focus on the classical teachings of witchcraft and ceremonial magic. It emphasizes the dynamic balance between the male and female divine energies, with the High Priest and High Priestess working in harmony during rituals and magical workings.

Alex Sanders' teachings revolve around the idea that magic should be accessible to all and should be harnessed for the betterment of one's own life and the lives of those around them.
