To begin with, the Hermetic principle of "As above, so below" is a fundamental concept in esoteric and occult studies, derived from the Emerald Tablet of Hermes Trismegistus. It postulates that everything that exists in the macrocosm (the greater universe) is a reflection of the microcosm (the individual) and vice versa. In this sense, our personal realities are a mirror of our inner worlds, and our manifested reality is shaped by our conscious intention and subconscious desires.

Conscious intention represents the directed thoughts and purposes we hold in our waking state. These intentions are our mental compass, guiding our actions and decisions towards set goals. However, conscious intention alone is often not sufficient to manifest desired outcomes, primarily because our subconscious desires play an equally powerful role in shaping our reality.

The subconscious mind is the storehouse of our deepest desires, beliefs, and memories. These aspects of ourselves are often hidden from our conscious awareness, revealing themselves through dreams, indirect actions, and intuitive insights. Manifested reality is a result of the interplay between conscious intention and subconscious desires. Understanding how these two aspects intertwine and influence each other is crucial for an adept on the path to realizing their true will.

To align conscious intention and subconscious desires for effective realization of one's true will, several steps can be followed.

1. **Self-examination and awareness**: The first step is to develop a heightened sense of self-awareness and introspection. By regularly engaging in practices such as meditation, journaling, and dream analysis, an adept can gain insights into their subconscious mind and bring clarity to their conscious intentions.

2. **Defining and refining the true will**: Through inner exploration, the adept must discern their genuine desires and goals, considering their talents, passions, and values in the broader context of cosmic unity. This understanding of one's true will should then be continuously refined and clarified as the practitioner grows and evolves spiritually.

For instance, consider a budding artist seeking to realize their true will. Their conscious intention might be to create meaningful and beautiful artwork, while upon introspection, they discover subconscious desires linked to seeking approval and validation from others. Identifying these conflicting motivations, the artist refines their true will to focus on self-expression, growth, and sharing beauty with the world, removing the desire for external validation.

3. **Alignment through intention and visualization**: To bring their conscious intention and subconscious desires into harmony, the adept must consistently reinforce their true will through affirmation, ritual, and visualization techniques. By repeatedly focusing on desired outcomes and emotions, they infuse these intentions into their subconscious, imprinting their true will and aligning their mindset for success.

4. **Taking aligned action**: With clear and purposeful intentions in place, the adept must take strategic actions aligned with their true will to manifest desired outcomes. As they make progress, synchronicities and meaningful coincidences may arise, indicating that their conscious intention, subconscious desires, and manifested reality are increasingly coming into alignment.

In summary, the Hermetic principle "As above, so below" suggests a profound interconnectedness between our inner worlds, comprising our conscious intentions and subconscious desires, and the reality we manifest. To effectively realize their true will, an adept must cultivate self-awareness, clarify their goals, instill alignment through intention and visualization, and take aligned action. By doing so, they will harmonize their thoughts, emotions, and actions, enabling the fruitful manifestation of their deepest desires and aspirations as a reflection of the cosmos itself.
