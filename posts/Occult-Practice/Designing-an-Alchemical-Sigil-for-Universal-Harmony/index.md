To fully tackle the challenge of crafting an elaborate sigil that combines the principles of the Hermetic axiom "As above, so below. As within, so without" with the core tenets of alchemy and their symbolism, we must begin by breaking down the constituent components of this task. 

The Hermetic Axiom states that there is a correspondence between the macrocosm (the universe) and the microcosm (the individual). This idea is encompassed by the expression "As above, so below. As within, so without", meaning that the workings of the universe are mirrored in our inner world, our thoughts, and emotions.

Alchemy, on the other hand, is a philosophical and protoscientific tradition aiming at the transmutation of base metals into noble metals, or more generally, the exploration of the relationship between the spiritual and material realms. It uses various symbols and principles to achieve this goal, including the four core elements (fire, water, air, earth) and their associated planetary symbols.

With this foundation in mind, we can now tackle the creation of the sigil that combines both the Hermetic axiom and Alchemical principles.
**Step 1**: Define the Intended Purpose
The intended purpose of the sigil will be to establish a connection between the practitioner's inner world (microcosm) and the external universe (macrocosm) to facilitate self-awareness, personal growth, and a deeper understanding of one's place within the cosmos. This sigil will aid the practitioner in tapping into universal wisdom and synchronizing their thoughts and actions with the natural harmony of the universe.
**Step 2**: Establish Planetary and Elemental Correspondences
Since we aim to combine planetary and elemental correspondences, we need to identify the relationships between the two. One way to do this is to use the traditional associations between the seven classical planets and the four elements:

1. Sun - Fire
2. Moon - Water
3. Mars - Fire
4. Mercury - Air/Water (due to its dual nature)
5. Jupiter - Air
6. Venus - Water
7. Saturn - Earth
**Step 3**: Design the Sigil
The design of the sigil can begin with the creation of a geometric structure that represents the macrocosm; in this case, an upward-facing triangle can be used, signifying the cosmos as a whole. The next step would be to place the symbols of the seven classical planets (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, and Saturn) around the border of this triangle.

To represent the microcosm - that is, the practitioner's inner world - a downward-facing triangle can be placed within the upward-facing triangle, forming a hexagram that signifies the unification of the macrocosm and microcosm. Finally, within this smaller, downward-facing triangle, the four elemental symbols can be arranged, with each one linked to an associated planet within the larger triangle.
**Step 4**: Application in Rituals and Meditation
In a ritual or meditation context, the practitioner would use this sigil as a focal point to synchronize their consciousness with the natural harmony of the universe. By focusing on the sigil, the practitioner connects with the energy flows of various planets and elements, allowing them to delve deeper into their own psyche, facilitate self-awareness, and access innate knowledge and wisdom. 

In conclusion, by combining both the Hermetic axiom "As above, so below. As within, so without" and alchemical principles, we can create an elaborate sigil that captures a holistic representation of the connection between the macrocosm and microcosm. This sigil is aimed at fostering personal growth and understanding one's place within the cosmos during rituals or meditation. It takes into account planetary and elemental correspondences, fusing them into a harmonious design that signifies the unity of the inner and outer worlds.
