To design a world reflecting the dimensions of spiritual ascent and descent within the realm of occultism and esotericism, we must first dive into the key concepts that form the backbone of this world. The three foundational systems that we'll draw upon are Tarot, chakras, and the elemental planes. 

Tarot is a system of divination that uses a deck of 78 cards, divided into the Major Arcana and Minor Arcana, each with its unique symbolism. The Major Arcana consists of 22 cards that depict the journey of spiritual growth, while the Minor Arcana consists of 56 cards representing various aspects of daily life, including emotions, desires, and aspirations. 

Chakras are the energy centers within the human body, according to various traditions of Indian metaphysical thought. There are typically seven chakras, each corresponding to a different level of consciousness and spiritual awareness. The chakras are aligned along the spinal column, with the root chakra at the lowest point and the crown chakra at the highest point.

Elemental planes, inspired by the classical elements of Earth, Water, Air, and Fire, represent the basic building blocks of reality. In many esoteric systems, these elements correspond to different dimensions of existence, with their unique properties and energies.

Bringing the elements of Tarot, chakras, and elemental planes together, we can create a world of spiritual ascent and descent with a rich narrative.

Our world consists of seven realms that mirror the levels of the chakras, starting with the Realm of Root at the base and culminating at the Divine Summit, which corresponds to the crown chakra. Within each realm, various civilizations, landscapes, and creatures showcase the elemental plane's properties, providing visual and experiential symbolism for the spiritual themes.

In each realm, individuals must overcome challenges and trials to spiritually ascend. These trials are carefully designed to correspond with specific cards in the Major Arcana, presenting the opportunity for personal growth in meaningful ways that align with a person's path. For example, the Realm of Root might have a trial that embodies the symbolism found in The Fool card, where one embarks on the journey without fear of the unknown.

As individuals progress through each realm and integrate the lessons of the Major Arcana, they must balance their emotions, desires, and aspirations, as reflected in the Minor Arcana. This balancing act ensures their grounding in reality while continuing to strive for higher levels of spiritual attainment.

The interplay between chakras, Tarot, and elemental planes creates a dynamic narrative that unfolds as individuals progress through the realms. Encountering diverse characters representing archetypes of the Tarot, the inhabitants of the elemental planes, and the rich cultures and spirituality rooted in the chakra system, the story evolves and grows richer.

Navigating through this world forces participants to confront the challenges that ultimately reflect their own spiritual journey. Spiritual growth in this world is thus not just a result of external experiences but a deeply personal exploration of one's psyche and innermost desires.

In conclusion, the world we've designed reflects the dimensions of spiritual ascent and descent by integrating Tarot, chakras, and elemental planes. It consists of seven realms corresponding to chakra levels, each imbued with the qualities of the elemental planes. Throughout this world, inhabitants face trials and challenges corresponding to the Major and Minor Arcana, fostering spiritual growth. These elements intertwine to create a rich narrative that unfolds within this world, and spiritual ascent and descent is not only a collective experience but a deeply personal journey, reflecting the individual's inner landscape.
