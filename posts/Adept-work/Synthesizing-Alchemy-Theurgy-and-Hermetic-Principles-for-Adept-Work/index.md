In response to your knowledge challenge, let us begin by establishing a solid foundation in understanding the core principles of alchemy, theurgy, and the seven Hermetic Principles. By synthesizing these concepts, we can create a comprehensive method of Adept work that bridges the gap between the microcosm and macrocosm and facilitates the harmonious connection you seek.

1. **Alchemy**: Alchemy is an ancient philosophical and protoscientific tradition aimed at transforming base metals into noble metals like gold and silver. However, alchemy goes beyond material transmutation. It is also concerned with personal transformation, refining the spiritual aspect of an individual. The ultimate goal is to achieve the Philosopher's Stone, not only as a physical substance but also as a metaphor for inner transformation and enlightenment.

2. **Theurgy**: Theurgy, derived from the Greek words "theos" (god) and "ergon" (work), refers to the practice of rituals and invocations to facilitate communion with divine beings. It is a means of elevating the soul and realizing a higher state of being through divine connection. Theurgy emphasizes the importance of developing inner virtues and purifying the self to establish a direct relationship with spiritual forces.

3. **The Seven Hermetic Principles**: The Hermetic Principles are a set of philosophical teachings attributed to Hermes Trismegistus, the Greek god of wisdom. These principles are believed to govern the natural world, underpinning the esoteric knowledge and offering a framework for understanding the universe. **The principles are as follows**:
 i. **Mentalism**: The universe is a mental construct, created by the All or the Infinite Mind. ii. **Correspondence**: The macrocosm is a reflection of the microcosm, and vice versa. iii. **Vibration**: Everything in existence vibrates and moves. iv. **Polarity**: Everything has its opposites, which are different aspects of the same thing. v. **Rhythm**: Everything moves in cycles and patterns. vi. **Cause and Effect**: Every cause has an effect, and every effect has a cause. vii. **Gender**: Everything has masculine and feminine aspects.

Now, synthesizing these concepts into a comprehensive method of Adept work, we can create a dynamic equilibrium between internal transformation and external manifestation.

First and foremost, an Adept must focus on personal transformation through alchemical practices. This process involves reflecting on one's inherent strengths and weaknesses and developing inner virtues to transmute base tendencies into noble qualities. In this context, the Philosopher's Stone represents the highest state of spiritual enlightenment, achieved by refining the soul.

Simultaneously, an Adept must engage in theurgical practices, invoking divine forces through rituals and invocations. By establishing a direct link with spiritual entities, the Adept can draw upon their wisdom and guidance to achieve a higher state of being.

The Seven Hermetic Principles provide the philosophical and practical framework to navigate the alchemical and theurgical practices. By understanding the interconnectedness of the macrocosm and microcosm (Correspondence), the Adept can align themselves with the universal laws governing the cosmos. By mastering the art of mental transmutation (Mentalism), they can alter their reality through focused intention. Embracing the principles of Polarity, Rhythm, Vibration, Cause and Effect, and Gender allows the Adept to harmonize with the intrinsic nature of the universe.

In conclusion, synthesizing the core principles of alchemy, theurgy, and the Hermetic Principles enables an individual to create a comprehensive method of Adept work. This methodology achieves a dynamic equilibrium between internal transformation and external manifestation. By refining the soul through alchemical practices, establishing a direct connection with spiritual forces through theurgical rituals, and aligning oneself with the universal laws that govern the cosmos, a harmonious connection between the microcosm and macrocosm can be established.
