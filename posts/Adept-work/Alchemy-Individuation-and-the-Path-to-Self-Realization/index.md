To answer this profoundly engaging knowledge challenge, let us first delve into the individual concepts mentioned: the Great Work in alchemy and Hermeticism, individuation in Jungian psychology, the stages of Nigredo, Albedo, and Rubedo, and the role they play in an Adept's path to achieving Kether in the Qabalistic Tree of Life.

The Great Work is the ultimate goal of alchemy and Hermeticism, a tradition aimed at the spiritual, mental, and physical transformation of the practitioner. The central idea is to create the philosopher's stone, which is seen as an enlightenment of the soul, a step on the path to immortality and a perfect understanding of the universe. In alchemy and Hermeticism, the Great Work is a complex process that often involves multiple stages, distinct symbols, and analogies derived from various sources, including religious, mythological, and philosophical texts.

Individuation, in Jungian psychology, is the process by which an individual becomes a psychologically integrated person by bringing together the disparate, and sometimes conflicting, elements of their unconscious and conscious minds. This self-realization is achieved over time and requires insight into both the bright and dark aspects of one's psyche. Jung borrowed from alchemical symbolism and the concept of the Great Work as a metaphorical framework to explain individuation.

Now, concerning the stages of Nigredo, Albedo, and Rubedo in alchemy, they represent essential steps in the transformational journey of the practitioner towards self-actualization. Nigredo, the blackening stage, signifies the decomposition and purification of matter. This stage corresponds to the descent into one's unconscious, confronting the shadow self, and recognizing the darkness within.

Albedo, the whitening stage, represents the rebirth of the purified material that emerges from the decay of Nigredo. In psychology, it corresponds to developing a more profound understanding of one's anima and animus (the feminine and masculine aspects within oneself). The initiate learns to acknowledge and connect with these aspects and achieve personal balance.

Rubedo, the reddening stage, is when the philosopher's stone – the final goal of the transformation – is formed. In psychological terms, it corresponds to the conscious integration and synthesis of all aspects of the self, symbolizing the highest level of self-realization. The individual becomes balanced, spiritually awakened, and reaches the peak of individuation.

Kether, the highest point on the Qabalistic Tree of Life, signifies the divine crown and the connection between the individual soul and the universal divinity. The Tree of Life is a map of spiritual progression, where one moves upwards through the sephiroth (spheres) to experience and embody higher states of consciousness. The stages of Nigredo, Albedo, and Rubedo can be seen as crucial steps in the Adept's journey on the path to achieving Kether. By undergoing these transformative stages, the Adept is brought closer to the divine source, achieving the state of unity and totality symbolized by Kether.

To summarize, the Great Work in alchemy and Hermeticism is a spiritual and mental process aimed at transformation, enlightenment, and immortality. The concept of individuation in Jungian psychology focuses on integrating the various aspects of the psyche to become a whole, mentally balanced person. The alchemical stages Nigredo, Albedo, and Rubedo correspond to the psychological processes involved in individuation, wherein each stage signifies different developmental phases of the transformative journey. Finally, these stages play a significant role in an Adept's path to achieving Kether on the Qabalistic Tree of Life, as they represent essential steps in the process of self-realization and divine unity.
