These archetypes are not only found in mythologies and folklore, but they also have an influence on art, literature, and psychology. The works of the Swiss psychiatrist Carl Gustav Jung made these archetypal concepts popular in the twentieth century.

The wise old man figure is an archetype that often appears as a mentor or guide, providing knowledge, guidance, and wisdom. This figure is repeated across various cultures and belief systems, reflecting a deep-rooted need for humans to seek wisdom and understanding from an external source. Some prominent examples of the wise old man are Merlin from Arthurian legends, Odin from Norse mythology, and Confucius in Chinese culture.
