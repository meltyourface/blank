To answer the knowledge challenge, let us begin by understanding the essential concepts of spiritual Alchemy and the Magnum Opus within the context of Aleister Crowley's Thelema, and then analyze how the stages of the Magnum Opus relate to one's personal journey towards self-realization in Thelema through True Will.

Aleister Crowley was a 20th-century occultist who founded the spiritual philosophy known as Thelema. Thelema is a system of belief with its foundation rooted in achieving one's True Will or ultimate purpose in life. Crowley linked his teachings to the ancient and mystical art of Alchemy, primarily spiritual Alchemy, which is often considered as an allegory for inner transformation and self-realization.

Spiritual Alchemy, in essence, is the process of taking raw, unrefined aspects of the self and converting them into spiritual gold through personal growth and development. The Magnum Opus, or Great Work, refers to the process of spiritual Alchemy wherein an individual, through various stages of purification and refinement, achieves a perfected spiritual state.

In the context of Aleister Crowley's Thelema, True Will defines an individual's innate purpose or mission in life, connected to their inmost nature. The True Will transcends societal norms and individual desires, operating at a deeper level of consciousness. According to Crowley, the rigors of spiritual Alchemy and the actualization of one's True Will are intrinsically linked through the stages of the Magnum Opus.

The Magnum Opus can be primarily condensed into four symbolic stages:

1. Nigredo (Blackening): In the initial stage of Alchemy, the individual is confronted with their base, impure essence. This stage is characterized by darkness, confusion, and suffering, leading the individual to a profound destruction of all previous illusions of self. Nigredo symbolizes the individual's awareness of their True Will as something distinct from their superficial desires and societal expectations. It represents the beginning of the journey towards self-realization in Thelema.

2. Albedo (Whitening): In this stage, the individual undergoes a purification process aimed at stripping away all impurities. Albedo represents the emergence of a newfound self-awareness and the beginning of spiritual growth. The individual starts to connect with the True Will at a deeper level, developing an understanding of their genuine desires and inherent potential beyond societal norms.

3. Citrinitas (Yellowing): This is where integration takes place, signifying spiritual enlightenment and intellectual clarity. In this phase, the individual synthesizes their newly discovered inner wisdom and understanding of their True Will with the outer world. It is a stage marked by the blossoming of creativity, self-expression, and spiritual development, allowing the individual to manifest their True Will in the external world.

4. Rubedo (Reddening): The final stage of the Magnum Opus represents the culmination of the individual's spiritual Alchemy process. Here, the individual merges the external world with their inner essence, fully realizing their True Will, achieving spiritual gold, and creating an authentic life imbued with meaning and purpose. Rubedo is the ultimate stage of self-realization in the context of Thelema.

In summary, Aleister Crowley's interpretation of spiritual Alchemy, particularly in his concept of True Will, showcases a transformative process that aligns an individual with their innermost desires and ultimate purpose in life. By undergoing the symbolic stages of the Magnum Opus (Nigredo, Albedo, Citrinitas, and Rubedo), an individual embarks on a journey of personal growth, spiritual enlightenment, and self-realization in Thelema. This journey serves as a means to understand oneself better, achieve the True Will, and ultimately, live a life of authenticity and harmony.
