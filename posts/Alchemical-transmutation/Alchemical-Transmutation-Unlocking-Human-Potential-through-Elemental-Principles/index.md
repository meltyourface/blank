In order to respond to this knowledge challenge, let us first understand the four elements and three principles of Alchemical transmutation. The four classical elements are Earth, Air, Fire, and Water, which represent different states of matter and are believed to be the building blocks of the universe. The three fundamental principles of alchemy are Sulphur, Mercury, and Salt, which represent the Spirit, Soul, and Body respectively. These principles act as essential components of the material world and are manifested within each of the four elements.

Now, let us explore the process of combining these elements and principles to create the Philosopher's Stone, a symbol of the ultimate achievement in Alchemy - the Magnum Opus or Great Work. The Philosopher's Stone is an object that bestows extraordinary powers such as transmutation of base metals into gold, the Elixir of Life conferring eternal youth, and spiritual enlightenment.

1. Calcination (Fire): The first step in the process involves breaking down the ego, symbolized by burning all that is impure in the self. This represents the earthly component, or the body (Salt). Calcination exposes our ego-driven fears, limitations, and desires, allowing us to let go of attachments and patterns that no longer serve us. Here, the element of Fire acts as a purifier and transformer.

2. Dissolution (Water): The next stage involves an emotional process, which is the dissolution of our unconscious beliefs, habits, and identities. This is associated with the fluid nature of the soul (Mercury) and the element Water, representing feelings and intuition. Dissolution allows us to immerse ourselves in the vast ocean of consciousness, embracing our vulnerability and surrendering to the unknown.

3. Separation (Air): The third step focuses on the mental aspect, where we separate and discern the various parts of our personality that require transformation. This stage correlates to the spirit (Sulphur) and the element Air, which represents thoughts and intellect. Separation entails an illumination of the mind, granting an understanding of who we truly are, along with a clearer perception of reality.

4. Conjunction (Earth): The final stage is the integration and unification of all the purified aspects of our being, symbolizing the birth of the Philosopher's Stone. The union of body (Salt), soul (Mercury), and spirit (Sulphur) brings about a divine harmony within ourselves and our surroundings. At this point, we have attained a higher state of consciousness and become one with Nature and the Cosmos, realizing our role in the microcosmic-macrocosmic relationship.

The significance of personal transformation in this process cannot be overemphasized. It is through challenging the limitations of our ego, emotions, thoughts, and intuition that we unlock the potential for growth, self-mastery, and enlightenment. The stages of Alchemical transmutation are not merely esoteric concepts, but rather a metaphorical blueprint for realizing our full potential and aligning with the universal harmony.

In summary, the Philosopher's Stone is a manifestation of the human potential achieved through combining the elements of Earth, Air, Fire, and Water, as well as the principles of Salt, Mercury, and Sulphur, in a transformative process. Personal transformation plays a vital role in wielding the power of the Philosopher's Stone and realizing the interconnectedness of the microcosmic and macrocosmic realms. The journey towards the Philosopher's Stone embodies a spiritual and psychological growth, fostering harmony within both the individual and the universe.
