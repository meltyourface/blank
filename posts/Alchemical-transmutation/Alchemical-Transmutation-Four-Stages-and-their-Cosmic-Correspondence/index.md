To embark on the journey of understanding the Magnum Opus's four stages — Nigredo, Albedo, Citrinitas, and Rubedo — and how they correspond to and influence the transmutation process of lead into gold, we must first define each stage and explore the philosophy behind them. Additionally, we will delve into the 'Filius Macrocosmi' concept, investigating its connection to these stages and the broader metaphysical implications and interpretations of the alchemist's journey towards self-discovery and spiritual enlightenment.

The Magnum Opus, or the Great Work, is the ultimate goal of the alchemical transmutation process. It represents the transformation not only of base metals into more noble forms but also of the alchemist's soul or consciousness. The four stages describe the roadmap towards achieving this aim through physical, psychological, and spiritual transformation.

1. Nigredo: The first stage, Nigredo, is often associated with the color black or a darkening process. At this stage, the objective is to break down and pulverize the base matter (i.e., lead) into its most primal form — a state of chaos and suffering. In a spiritual sense, this stage correlates to the alchemist's confrontation with their shadowy, unconscious aspects, bringing them to light for introspection and understanding.

2. Albedo: Following the darkening process of Nigredo, Albedo introduces a sense of purification. The alchemical substance becomes white or silver, symbolizing the removal of impurities. Here, the separation of opposing elements takes place, such as male and female, good and evil, light and darkness. This stage corresponds to the alchemist's self-awareness and integration of duality, fostering balance between their conscious and unconscious selves.

3. Citrinitas: This is the stage of ‘yellowing’ or illumination, marking the alchemist's understanding of the sacred nature of their work. The substance turns yellow or golden, reflecting an advancement in the transmutation process. In the context of personal development, Citrinitas represents the awakening of the alchemist’s authentic inner wisdom and the recognition of their true nature.

4. Rubedo: The final stage, Rubedo, is the culmination of the previous steps and is represented by the color red or sometimes even purple. It signifies the successful union of opposites, the merging of the purified consciousness, and the creation of the Philosopher's Stone, the instrument capable of transforming base metals into gold. For the alchemist, this stage embodies the realization of their inherent divine nature and the attainment of spiritual enlightenment.

Now, let us explore the 'Filius Macrocosmi' concept – the notion that the alchemical process mirrors the microcosm of human existence within the macrocosm of the universe. This understanding suggests that the stages of the Magnum Opus serve not only as a guide to the transmutation of metals but also as a spiritual parallel for the alchemist's personal journey.

Influencing the transmutation of lead into gold, these stages reflect the need for destruction, purification, and reconstruction. Breaking down the lead's structure and removing impurities parallels the breaking down of ego and self-imposed limitations. The purified golden state achieved through the Magnum Opus signifies the unearthing of intrinsic virtues, personal transformation, and a newfound connection to the universe's macrocosmic wisdom.

In conclusion, the four stages of the Magnum Opus, alongside the 'Filius Macrocosmi' concept, play a significant role in the transmutation process of lead into gold. They provide a roadmap for this transformation, with each stage contributing to the progression and refinement of the substance being worked upon. Additionally, these stages signify the path towards self-discovery and spiritual enlightenment, with metaphysical implications that extend beyond mere laboratory procedures. In essence, the journey of the Magnum Opus encapsulates the belief that through the alchemical process, one can attain not only material wealth but also spiritual riches.
