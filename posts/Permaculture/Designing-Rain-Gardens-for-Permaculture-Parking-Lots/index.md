- Pervious concrete: 0.1 - 0.5
- Porous asphalt: 0.15 - 0.7
- Permeable pavers: 0.15 - 0.7

In a permaculture-designed parking lot, the vegetation may include trees, shrubs, and groundcovers. The vegetation density can contribute to reducing the runoff coefficient by 10% - 40%.

3. Determine the location-based coefficient: Climate, slope, and overall landscape can affect the runoff coefficient. For example, areas with heavy rainfall or steep slopes may have higher runoff coefficients.

4. Consider water flow velocity: A well-designed permaculture parking lot will encourage slower water flow, allowing more time for infiltration and reduced runoff.

5. Calculate the combined runoff coefficient: The specific runoff coefficient for the permaculture-designed parking lot can be calculated by combining the factors mentioned above.
