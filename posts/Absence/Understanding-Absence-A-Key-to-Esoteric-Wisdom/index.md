To answer this knowledge challenge, we must first delve into the paradoxical nature of absence as both a void and a presence in occult and esoteric studies. This will involve drawing from the principles of Hermeticism, Kabbalah, and Eastern spiritual traditions. Then, we will explore how concealment and revelation of esoteric wisdom within the absence leads to the transformative understanding of the initiates on their path to enlightenment.

In Hermeticism, the principle of correspondence states, "As above, so below; as below, so above." This maxim suggests that the microcosm mirrors the macrocosm and vice versa. Within this context, absence can be seen as both a void, representing the infinite potential of creation, and a presence, encompassing the manifested universe. Hermetic teachings emphasize that understanding the universe involves unraveling the paradox of absence, which ultimately leads to the comprehension of divine wisdom.

The Kabbalah, a mystical interpretation of Judaism, deals with the concept of absence through the notion of Ein Sof, which translates to "no end" or "infinity." Ein Sof is both the infinite light of God and the void from which the creation emerges. Kabbalists believe that understanding the paradoxical duality of Ein Sof—the simultaneous presence and absence—is integral to understanding the divine and the purpose of creation.

Eastern spiritual traditions, like Hinduism, Buddhism, and Taoism, also consider absence in various forms. For example, Hinduism's concept of Brahman, the absolute universal spirit, is both the source and manifestation of everything in existence. Similarly, Taoism revolves around the Tao, which can be interpreted as the indefinable force that transcends duality and embodies both presence and absence. In Buddhism, Sunyata (emptiness) is a key concept, which refers to the absence of intrinsic nature in all phenomena, yet is also seen as the ultimate reality.

To address the issue of reconciling the simultaneous concealment and revelation of esoteric wisdom within the absence, we must explore its transformative potential. Initiation and the process of enlightenment are founded on the recognition of absence as both a presence and a void. The adept must learn to recognize the divine spark within this space, discovering the interconnectivity between all things.

Throughout the transformative journey, initiates undergo a deeply personal experience of absence. In this process, they confront their subjective realities, expanding their consciousness, and dismantling the illusion of separation. As they do so, the veil between the concealed and the revealed begins to thin, enabling the acquisition of hidden esoteric wisdom.

In conclusion, the paradoxical nature of absence as both a void and a presence is an essential concept within Hermeticism, Kabbalah, and Eastern spiritual traditions. The reconciliation of this paradox leads to an understanding of divine wisdom and creation itself, representing the symbolic path towards enlightenment for initiates. By grasping the nature of this paradox and undergoing a personal transformation, the initiate becomes capable of perceiving the concealed knowledge within the absence, leading to a more profound understanding of the esoteric and the divine. Ultimately, the initiates' journey toward enlightenment is characterized by a deepening awareness of the intricate interplay between presence and absence, concealment and revelation.
