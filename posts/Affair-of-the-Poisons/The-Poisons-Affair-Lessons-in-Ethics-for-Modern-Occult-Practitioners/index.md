## The Affair of the Poisons - Historical Background, Key Figures, and Implications for the Modern Occult Student

### I. Historical Background

The Affair of the Poisons is a dark and intriguing episode in the history of France, unfolding during the rule of King Louis XIV between 1677 and 1682. As cases of poisonings multiplied, authorities began to investigate and eventually unearthed a network of fortune-tellers, alchemists, and poisoners operating within the heart of Paris. This discovery revealed extensive dealings in love potions, poisons, and other forms of occult practices, many of which were engaged by members of high society.

Among the key figures involved in the Affair of the Poisons were:

2. Catherine Deshayes Monvoisin, known as La Voisin: A fortune-teller and poisoner who played a central role in the Affair. She was involved in creating and selling poisons and facilitating black masses for her high-profile clients.

3. **Madame de Montespan**: One of King Louis XIV's most well-known mistresses, who was implicated in the Affair but ultimately protected by the King. She allegedly sought La Voisin's services to create love potions to maintain the King's affection or to dispose of royal rivals.

During the period of the Affair of the Poisons, several types of potions and poisons were prevalent, such as:

1. **Aqua Tofana**: A slow-acting, almost undetectable poison made from arsenic, lead, and belladonna. It was highly sought after by those wishing to eliminate their enemies discreetly.

2. **Love Potions and Philters**: These concoctions were intended to induce or enhance romantic feelings in their intended targets. They typically consisted of ingredients like herbs, flowers, and animal parts with supposed aphrodisiac properties.

3. **Powders of Succession**: This insidious mixture was a poison designed to slowly weaken its victim, allowing the poisoner to inherit property or titles from the deceased.
