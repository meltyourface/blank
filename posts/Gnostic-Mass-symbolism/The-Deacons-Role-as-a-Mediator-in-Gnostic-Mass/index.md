**Introduction**:
The Gnostic Mass, also known as Liber XV or The Ecclesia Gnostica Catholica, is a ritualized enactment of spiritual concepts and symbols designed to bring about individual and collective transcendence. Among the key ritual participants, the Deacon occupies a critical position as a mediator between various polarities such as spirit and matter, authority and servitude, and macrocosm and microcosm.

During the Gnostic Mass, the Deacon serves as a bridge between the primary celebrant, the Priest, and the congregation. They act as an intermediary, facilitating communication and symbolically connecting the various roles and elements within the Mass. The Deacon also is responsible for guiding the congregation through the ritual, proclaiming the sacred texts, and assisting with the administration of sacraments. The Deacon's role as a mediator reflects their overarching function and importance within the ceremony as they make connections between the aforementioned polarities.
