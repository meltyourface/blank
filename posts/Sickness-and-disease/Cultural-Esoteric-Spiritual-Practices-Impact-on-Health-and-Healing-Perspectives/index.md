An example scenario is the use of shamanism in certain indigenous cultures. In this case, society's belief in the powers of the shaman led to an interconnected view of illness as not solely bodily afflictions, but also as spiritual imbalances or the result of malevolent entities. Consequently, the shaman's role in addressing illness went beyond the physical realm to involve esoteric practices, like journeying to the spirit world and communicating with spirits.

The relationship between esoteric spiritual practices and a holistic understanding of health can also influence the perception and treatment of diseases. In ancient Chinese culture, the principles of balance and harmony emphasized in the esoteric philosophy of Taoism informed their understanding of health and illness. The concept of Yin and Yang, representing complementary and opposing energies, highlighted the need for equilibrium within the body to maintain health. Moreover, the idea of Chi (life force energy) that flows through the body was crucial in determining the health of an individual. Blockages in this flow could lead to illness, and it was the task of traditional Chinese medicine practitioners to unblock and realign the flow of Chi through acupuncture, herbal remedies, and other techniques.
