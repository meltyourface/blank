To fully grasp the interrelation between the principles of correspondence, vibration, and causation in the context of Naturalistic Occultism, it is crucial to first understand each principle individually, and then recognize how they intertwine and connect. Finally, we will explore how integrating these principles into one's personal practice can lead to deeper understanding and transformation.

1. The Principle of Correspondence
The principle of correspondence is often encapsulated by the phrase "as above, so below; as within, so without." It implies that there is a pattern or correspondence between the microcosm and the macrocosm, between an individual's inner experiences and the outer world. In Naturalistic Occultism, this principle suggests that by studying the natural world and the laws governing it, one can develop a deeper understanding of one's own inner nature.

2. The Principle of Vibration
The principle of vibration postulates that everything in existence is in a constant state of motion or oscillation, from the smallest subatomic particle to the vast expanse of the cosmos. This constant state of movement and change is an intrinsic characteristic of energy, and energy is the fundamental building block of all matter and phenomena in the universe. In the context of Naturalistic Occultism, this principle implies that by attuning oneself to the vibrational frequencies of the natural world, one can work towards harmony and resonance with its energy patterns, which in turn leads to personal growth and transformation.

3. The Principle of Causation
The principle of causation espouses the idea that every action, thought, or event is the result of a particular cause or set of causes. There is an interconnected web of causality in the universe, which means that everything is interrelated through cause and effect relationships. In the context of Naturalistic Occultism, this principle encourages a thorough examination of one's thoughts, actions, and experiences, to understand the underlying causes and motives that drive them and to appreciate the consequences they leave on the self and the world at large.

The Interrelation of These Three Principles

Now that we have a basic understanding of each principle, it becomes clear how they relate to one another and intertwine within the framework of Naturalistic Occultism. The principle of correspondence teaches us that by observing and understanding the patterns in the natural world, we can gain insight into our own internal processes. In this context, the principle of vibration helps us attune to the oscillating energy patterns in the world, aligning our own energies with those natural patterns. Doing so allows us to create a harmonious and balanced state of being, capable of enacting positive changes in ourselves and in our environment.

Conversely, the principle of causation reminds us that our thoughts and actions have consequences, rippling across the interconnected web of existence. Thus, as we align our energies with the natural world, we must be mindful of the causes we set in motion and the results they engender. In this way, these three principles intertwine to form a holistic approach to self-improvement and development, acknowledging the interconnectedness of all things and emphasizing the importance of harmony and balance in our personal journey.

Applying These Principles to Transformative Practice

In order to utilize these principles in one's personal understanding and transformative practice, one must first focus on attuning oneself to the patterns and vibrations of the natural world. This can be achieved through various techniques, such as meditation, mindfulness, sensory awareness, and engaging with the elements (earth, air, water, fire).

Once attuned, individuals should contemplate how their thoughts and actions align with these observed patterns. Are they contributing to harmony and balance, or are they causing discord and instability? By carefully considering the impact of one's actions and thoughts on the world and on oneself, one can make informed decisions about how best to adjust their behavior and thoughts to achieve personal transformation and growth.

In conclusion, the principles of correspondence, vibration, and causation in the context of Naturalistic Occultism highlight the interconnectedness of everything in existence and the importance of harmony and balance in achieving personal growth and transformation. By internalizing these principles and actively applying them to one's own practice, an individual can work towards developing a deeper understanding of themselves and their place within the intricately interconnected web of existence. This, in turn, leads to positive change and improvement, both internally and externally.
