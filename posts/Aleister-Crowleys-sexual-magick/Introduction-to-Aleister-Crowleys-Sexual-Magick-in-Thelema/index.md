Aleister Crowley's Sexual Magick: An Overview

### I. History of Aleister Crowley's Sexual Magick

Aleister Crowley, born in 1875, was an influential occultist, poet, and mountaineer who founded the religion of Thelema. His philosophy revolved around the dictum "Do what thou wilt shall be the whole of the Law." 

Sexual magick played a pivotal role in Crowley's teachings and practice. Influenced by the works of Eliphas Levi, Paschal Beverly Randolph, and Hindu and Buddhist Tantra, Crowley integrated these principles to craft his own system of sexual magick.

### II. Key Principles and Practices

At the heart of Crowley's sexual magick is the belief that sexual energy can be harnessed and utilized to attain a higher level of spiritual consciousness and to manifest one's will. **There are two primary means of achieving this**: ritual and non-ritual sexual magick.

1. **Ritual Sexual Magick**: Specific rituals, such as the Gnostic Mass and the Lesser and Greater Mysteries of Sexual Magick, involve the consensual participation of practitioners in a controlled ceremony. These rituals focus on the use of sexual energy, symbolism, and visualization to achieve desired outcomes.

2. **Non-Ritual Sexual Magick**: Involves the use of sexual energy outside the confines of a formal ritual. Practitioners may engage in various techniques to enhance awareness and direct their energy towards a specific outcome, such as meditation, conscious breathing, and visualization.

### III. Thelema and Sexual Magick

In Crowley's Thelema, sexual magick is considered the highest form of magick, representing the union of opposites (male and female energies) and the symbolic joining of the microcosm (individual) with the macrocosm (cosmos). 

The practice of sexual magick in Thelema also emphasizes the importance of spiritual transformation and the evolution of the individual's consciousness. This development is intrinsically linked to the concept of the True Will, which refers to the Divine purpose of an individual.

### IV. Tantra and Crowley's Sexual Magick

Crowley's understanding of sexual magick was significantly informed by the teachings of Hindu and Buddhist Tantra. Tantra emphasizes the integration of the physical, sexual, and spiritual aspects of life and advocates for the cultivation of Divine energy through ritual and meditative practices. Crowley fused these elements with his own esoteric concepts to create a unique system of sexual magick.
