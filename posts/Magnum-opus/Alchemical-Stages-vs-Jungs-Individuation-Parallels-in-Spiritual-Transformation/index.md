This allows us to establish an understanding and build parallels between the two frameworks, ultimately revealing an integrative and comprehensive understanding of the transformative inner journey required for spiritual enlightenment.

Alchemical Stages:
1. Nigredo: This is the initial stage of alchemical transformation, also known as the blackening stage. It involves the breakdown of substances into their primal matter, characterized by feelings of darkness, confusion, and disorientation.
2. Albedo: Following nigredo, albedo is the purification or whitening stage. It represents the purification of primal matter, rendered brighter and clearer as it undergoes distillation and dissolution processes.
3. Citrinitas: The yellowing or citrine stage, citrinitas pertains to the integration of the purified matter into a new, harmonious whole, paving the way for enlightenment.
4. Rubedo: The final stage of alchemical transformation, rubedo is the reddening stage, representing the achievement of spiritual enlightenment and the unification of the spiritual and material realms.

Jung's Process of Individuation:
1. Shadow: The first stage in Jung's process involves recognizing and integrating the repressed or unconscious aspects of oneself, which he termed the "shadow." This step may manifest through introspection, experience, and confrontation of one's darker nature.
2. Anima/Animus: The next phase focuses on integrating the contrasexual aspects of the psyche, with anima representing the feminine characteristics in men, and animus the masculine characteristics in women. This enables the individual to establish a balance between their masculine and feminine energies.
3. The Wise Old Man/Woman Archetype: As the process continues, an individual will encounter the wisdom archetype, often represented as a wise old man or woman. This imparted knowledge and guidance contribute to one's spiritual growth.

Now, let's draw the parallels between the alchemical stages and the stages of individuation:

1. Nigredo & Shadow: Both signify the initial stages of transformation, where an individual confronts and acknowledges their darkness, confusion and disorientation.
2. Albedo & Anima/Animus: Here, there is purification or clarification resulting from integrating aspects of the psyche, such as the contrasexual components of the anima/animus in individuation, and primal matter in alchemy.
3. Citrinitas & Wise Old Man/Woman Archetype: Both stages represent the integration of wisdom and harmony, cultivating the foundation necessary for spiritual growth.
4. Rubedo & The Self: These stages signify the pinnacle of inner transformation, the achievement of spiritual enlightenment, and the unification of the spiritual and material realms.

In conclusion, the parallels and symbolic connections between alchemical stages and the psychological process of individuation proposed by Carl Jung underscore the intertwined nature of both frameworks in fostering a profound understanding of the transformative inner journey required for spiritual enlightenment. Both alchemy and Jung's process of individuation involve the confrontation and integration of dark elements within the self, followed by clarification and wisdom cultivation, culminating in the realization of spiritual enlightenment and unification of disparate aspects of the individual psyche.
