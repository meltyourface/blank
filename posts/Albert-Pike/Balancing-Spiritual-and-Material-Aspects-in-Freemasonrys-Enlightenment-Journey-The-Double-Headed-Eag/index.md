The "Double-headed Eagle of Lagash" is an ancient Sumerian symbol, attributed to the city-state of Lagash, which is believed to represent the concept of dualism or balance. The double-headed eagle has since been co-opted by numerous empires and organizations, including Freemasonry, to symbolize a concordance between two seemingly opposite powers, such as spiritual and material aspects.

Conversely, the right head of the eagle personifies the material aspects of Freemasonry, such as taking action in the world to enact positive change, benefiting society, and maintaining social order. The material aspect is concerned with the external, objective experiences in life, which often involve dealing with the circumstances of reality and addressing societal needs through charitable works and acts of service.

He believed that Enlightenment, which is the ultimate goal of Freemasonic practice, could only be achieved when a harmonious equilibrium between spiritual and material aspects is maintained.
