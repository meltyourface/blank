Answering this knowledge challenge requires a deep understanding of the intricacies of various esoteric and occult traditions, as well as the ability to synthesize and integrate diverse elements into a coherent and meaningful system.

To begin, let's briefly review the core components of a liturgical approach to transformation and illumination: symbolism, ritualistic elements, and sacred texts.

Symbolism is a crucial aspect of esoteric and occult teachings, as it communicates profound ideas through allegory and metaphor. Symbolism is particularly important in esotericism since these teachings are considered to be "hidden," and thus require a greater degree of interpretation and intuition.

Ritualistic elements are an established means for effecting personal transformation, using repeated symbolic actions and words to engage with the subtle forces of consciousness and the universe.

Sacred texts serve as repositories of knowledge, containing wisdom from diverse traditions that, when studied and engaged with, can provide significant insight into the spiritual journey.

To create a holistic and interconnected framework for personal transformation and spiritual illumination, let's develop an original liturgical approach that combines these three elements in the following manner:

Phase 1: Foundational Study
The initiate begins by studying texts from a diverse range of esoteric traditions. These texts should expose the reader to various cosmologies, creation myths, divine hierarchies, and principles of human spiritual development. Additionally, the initiate must learn about the symbolism inherent to each tradition, understanding how symbols convey meaning across different systems.

Phase 2: Identification of Commonalities
Once the individual has gained a foundational understanding of various traditions, they must begin to identify common themes, principles, and symbols that recur across the chosen systems. For instance, the initiate may recognize that concepts such as the Tree of Life and the interplay of opposites (light/dark, masculine/feminine) are prevalent across numerous esoteric systems.

Phase 3: Synthesis and Integration
Having identified the common threads, the practitioner can then create a synthesis of these ideas into an inclusive and coherent system that respects the integrity of each tradition while preserving harmony. This synthesis should provide a map for personal transformation and illumination that aligns with a specific set of principles and truths.

Phase 4: Developing Ritual Practices
With the integration of various traditions and the establishment of a synthesized system, the individual should create a guide or manual for ritual practices based on their understanding of the selected traditions. These rituals must incorporate symbolism and teachings from each tradition in a manner that reflects the harmonized, overarching system.

Phase 5: Personal Transformation and Illumination
Lastly, the practitioner should engage in the prescribed rituals and practices, using them as tools for personal transformation and illumination. By consistently participating in these rituals and reflecting on their symbolic significance, the individual will develop a deeper understanding of themselves and their place in the cosmic order.

In summary, an original liturgical approach to spiritual transformation and illumination should begin by studying a diverse range of esoteric traditions, identifying common themes and symbols, and then synthesizing these elements into a coherent and harmonious system. The individual should then develop ritual practices that reflect this integrated framework and engage regularly with them to promote personal growth and spiritual illumination. Throughout each step, great care must be taken to maintain the integrity and harmony of the traditions being combined, with an emphasis on understanding the esoteric wisdom each system imparts.
