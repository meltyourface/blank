To respond to the knowledge challenge, let us begin by breaking down the key components to be addressed: the concept of "As Above, So Below," subjective and objective reality, elemental correspondences, archetypal symbolism, teachings of major esoteric traditions, and achieving a transformative understanding of the fabric of reality.

"As Above, So Below" is derived from the ancient Hermetic axiom, “That which is Below corresponds to that which is Above, and that which is Above corresponds to that which is Below, to accomplish the miracle of the One Thing.” This principle suggests that the universe is interconnected, and the microcosm reflects the macrocosm and vice versa. It is a fundamental idea in various esoteric, occult, and mystical traditions.

Subjective reality refers to individual perception and experience, while objective reality consists of empirical truths that remain consistent across different observers. The concept of "As Above, So Below" is often used to indicate that the patterns observed in subjective experiences reveal the underlying principles of objective reality.

Elemental correspondences refer to the relationships between the four classical elements (earth, air, fire, and water) and aspects of reality. These elements are often used symbolically to represent different states of matter or energy, and they have associations with various human traits and emotional states.

Meanwhile, archetypal symbolism represents universal themes and images found in the collective unconscious, as proposed by Carl Jung. Archetypes are characters, symbols, or themes rooted deeply in the human psyche that emerge across cultures and time.

Major esoteric traditions include Hermeticism, Gnosticism, Kabbalah, and various mystical branches of major religions, such as Christian Mysticism and Sufism, all of which seek to uncover hidden knowledge about the nature of reality and the individual’s role within it.

To synthesize these key elements and address the challenge, let's first understand that "As Above, So Below" connects macrocosm and microcosm, implying that both subjective and objective reality share the same fundamental principles. The subjective experiences of an individual (Below) reflect the archetypal patterns and universal truths at the core of the objective reality (Above).

Elemental correspondences serve as a bridge between subjective and objective realities, offering tangible ways to understand and manipulate the world. For instance, applying the elemental framework to personal emotions and experiences allows the initiate to observe their links with wider natural processes and universal themes, ultimately fostering a deeper understanding of themselves and the world around them.

Major esoteric traditions incorporate the principle of "As Above, So Below" and use elemental correspondences and archetypal symbolism as critical tools to explore reality. For example, the Kabbalistic Tree of Life represents a map of the universe and the human soul, linking the individual's experiences to the larger cosmic structure, while alchemy embodies the transforming nature of reality through the elemental stages of transmutation.

Through the study of major esoteric traditions, initiates can develop a more profound understanding of reality's fabric by recognizing the interplay between subjective and objective realms. By deciphering the symbolic language of archetypes and elements and integrating the teachings of these mystical paths, an initiate can achieve a transformative understanding of reality and their place within it.

In conclusion, the concept of "As Above, So Below" applies to the nature of subjective and objective reality by suggesting that the principles governing both realms are interconnected. Elemental correspondences and archetypal symbolism provide practical tools for the initiate to perceive the patterns present in both the microcosm and macrocosm. By incorporating the teachings of major esoteric traditions, the initiate can attain a comprehensive and transformative understanding of the fabric of reality, ultimately transcending the boundaries between the inner and outer worlds.
