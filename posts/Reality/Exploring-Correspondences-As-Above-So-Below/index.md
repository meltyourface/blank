In order to answer this knowledge challenge, we must first understand the concept of "As Above, So Below" and delve into its implications and applications in the nature of subjective and objective reality. The concept of "As Above, So Below" originated from the Hermetic principle, which is a foundational tenet of Western esoteric tradition. This principle establishes a principle of correspondence, asserting that whatever happens on one plane of existence has a corresponding influence or manifestation on other planes of existence. Essentially, it conveys the idea that the microcosm is a reflection of the macrocosm, and vice versa.

Now, let us break down the different components of the challenge and explore their relationships with the concept of "As Above, So Below":

1. **Subjective and Objective Reality**: The concept of subjective reality relates to an individual's perception, emotions, and thoughts, while objective reality refers to the world as it exists independent of our perception. The principle of "As Above, So Below" can be applied to this dichotomy by suggesting that our subjective experience of reality is a reflection of the objective universe. Therefore, understanding this interconnectedness can provide insights into the nature of reality and guide the initiate towards a more profound comprehension of the cosmos.

2. **Elemental Correspondences**: Elements have been central to various esoteric traditions, as they represent the building blocks from which the material world is constructed. Typically, there are four fundamental elements - fire, water, air, and earth - which have been associated with various aspects of human life, and even personality traits. The concept of "As Above, So Below" influences elemental correspondences by suggesting that these elements are not simply static properties but have a corresponding relationship with the larger, cosmic processes. For instance, the element fire on the macrocosmic level could correspond to the solar processes that fuel life on Earth, while on the microcosmic level, it might correspond to human creativity, passion, and energy.

3. **Archetypal Symbolism**: Archetypes are universally recognized symbols or patterns representing essential human experiences. These symbols take various forms, such as deities or mythological figures, recurring motifs in literature, and universal themes across cultures. The Hermetic principle of "As Above, So Below" informs the study of archetypal symbolism by pointing to the interconnectedness between the individual psychological realm and the broader cosmos. By grasping these archetypes' microcosmic and macrocosmic correspondences, the initiate can assimilate practical wisdom and guidance to navigate life's challenges.

4. **Teachings of Major Esoteric Traditions**: Esoteric traditions, such as Hermeticism, Qabalah, Gnosticism, and Neoplatonism, explore the hidden aspects of reality, metaphysics, and cosmology. All these traditions emphasize the interconnectedness of reality and the importance of symbolism, either implicitly or explicitly encompassing the principle of "As Above, So Below." By studying these teachings and uncovering their links to this principle, the initiate can begin to synthesize seemingly disparate esoteric knowledge and reach a more comprehensive understanding of the fabric of reality.

In conclusion, the principle of "As Above, So Below" serves as a guiding framework for deeper comprehension of the interconnected nature of subjective and objective reality. By incorporating elemental correspondences, archetypal symbolism, and the teachings of major esoteric traditions, the initiate can embark on a transformative journey towards a more holistic and integrated worldview. Key themes encompassed in this knowledge challenge include the principle of correspondence, the dual nature of reality, the significance of elements and archetypes, and the relevance of esoteric teachings in fostering a profound understanding of the cosmos.
