**Explanation**:

When delving into the esoteric and occult practices, it is crucial to understand that symbolism and correspondences are an essential component when setting up an altar. Altar symbols are meticulously chosen by practitioners to fortify their spiritual practices and create a strong connection to the energies they aim to evoke or work with throughout their rituals.

Each symbol, color, and element on an altar holds its unique significance and carries a specific intention within the practice. Aligning one's altar with these traditional associations can noticeably enhance the atmosphere and efficacy of the rituals being conducted, as they act as conduits to channel and direct energy properly.

The other options are either incomplete or misguided approaches:

- Choosing symbols solely based on personal aesthetics, without consideration for their traditional meanings and associations, may result in a less potent and effective working due to lack of intentional alignment with the symbols' inherent energies.

- Focusing solely on the functionality of each tool without considering symbolism overlooks the power that symbolic associations contribute to an occult practice. By ignoring the meanings behind an object's design, practitioners may limit the effectiveness of their rituals and the depth of connection to the energies and entities they intend to work with.

- Utilizing only a set of universally approved symbols does not account for the personal experiences, cultural background, and individualized paths taken by practitioners within the occult. Acknowledging and incorporating personal and cultural elements may serve to further strengthen and invigorate the bond between practitioner and their work.

Related concepts essential for a thorough understanding of altar symbols in occult practices include the study of the four classical elements (earth, air, fire, and water), planetary associations, and the teachings of various cultural and historical magical traditions, such as Hermeticism, Kabbalah, or contemporary systems like Wicca or chaos magick. Comprehension of these underlying principles and the interplay of symbolism within the occult grants a robust foundation for the proper selection and arrangement of altar symbols, consequently maximizing the effectiveness of one's spiritual practice.
