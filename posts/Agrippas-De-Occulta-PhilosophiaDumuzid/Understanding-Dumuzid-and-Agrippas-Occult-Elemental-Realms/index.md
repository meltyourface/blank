The foundational tripartite structure of Agrippa's De Occulta Philosophia is composed of three books, each focusing on different aspects of the occult and mystical philosophy. These books are as follows:

1. Book One - Natural Magic: This section deals with the elemental forces of nature and their inter-connected relationships. It delves into the understanding of the four classical elements, that is, Earth, Water, Air, and Fire, and their governing principles. Agrippa explores how these elements can be manipulated and controlled through natural magic and provides insight into the process of understanding and utilizing their inherent mystical properties.

2. Book Two - Celestial Magic: This part of Agrippa's work focuses on the heavenly realms and the influence of celestial bodies over human lives. It discusses the significance of astrology, planetary magic, and the spiritual symbolism associated with various celestial beings, thus providing insight into the interconnected relationships between the macrocosm (the universe) and the microcosm (the human being).

3. Book Three - Ceremonial Magic: The final section of De Occulta Philosophia is centered on the intricate art of ceremonial magic, involving theurgy and ritualistic practices. This book delves into the more complex aspects of the occult, such as the invocation of spirits, the creation of talismans, and the establishment of various symbolic connections that enable a deeper understanding of the esoteric realm.
