Edmund Husserl's Phenomenology of Internal Time-Consciousness:
Edmund Husserl, a German philosopher, was the founder of phenomenology, and his work on the Phenomenology of Internal Time-Consciousness is regarded as a central concept in his philosophy. According to Husserl, time-consciousness is an intrinsic aspect of human experience that cannot be separated from other experiences. He emphasized the significance of time and its role in organizing the structure of consciousness.

For Husserl, the flow of time-consciousness consists of three main elements: retention, primal impression, and protention. Retention represents the past, or how we are continuously aware of past experiences. Primal impression refers to the present, or the immediate awareness of the current experience. Lastly, protention represents the future, or the anticipation of upcoming experiences.
