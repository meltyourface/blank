To thoroughly answer this knowledge challenge, we must first understand the key concepts involved, including The Kybalion, the principles it contains, spiritual alchemy, and the role of intention and mindfulness. Once we have established a clear understanding of these ideas, we can then analyze the ethical implications and limitations of reading thoughts in the context of these principles.

The Kybalion is a philosophical text, believed to have been written in early 20th century, that explores the hermetic principles governing the universe. The book presents these principles as the fundamental forces at work in our lives. Though the Kybalion explores seven hermetic principles, we will focus on the three most relevant to this challenge: The Principle of Mentalism, The Principle of Correspondence, and The Principle of Cause and Effect.

1. The Principle of Mentalism: This principle states that "All is Mind," and the universe is a mental construct in which everything that occurs is a result of thought. Consequently, our thoughts play a crucial role in shaping our reality and experiences.

2. The Principle of Correspondence: The famous maxim "As above, so below" encapsulates this principle. It suggests that there is a correspondence between different planes of existence, and the patterns found in one can be applied to another. This allows us to understand the greater cosmos through the study of our inner world.

3. The Principle of Cause and Effect: This principle proposes that every cause has its effect, and every effect has its cause. Nothing exists or occurs by chance, and there is a reason behind everything that happens in the universe.

Spiritual alchemy is the process of personal transformation and self-realization and can be divided into three stages: Nigredo (darkness), Albedo (purification), and Rubedo (union).

1. Nigredo: The individual recognizes their ignorance and the necessity for change. This stage is often marked by a sense of disillusionment and the realization of one's own limitations.

2. Albedo: The individual undergoes a process of purification, where they confront their inner flaws, weaknesses, and false beliefs to cultivate positive qualities and values.

3. Rubedo: The final stage involves the integration of one's newfound knowledge and enlightenment into everyday life, leading to the union of the individual's divine and human aspects.

Now that we have established an understanding of these concepts, we can begin to analyze the ethical implications and limitations of reading thoughts.

Mind reading involves accessing another individual's thoughts, thus potentially infringing upon their free will and privacy. This raises several ethical concerns, particularly when considering the aforementioned principles.

In the context of The Principle of Mentalism, accessing someone's thoughts means tapping into the very essence of their reality, which can have profound consequences on both the person whose thoughts are being read and the individual engaging in the practice. It is essential to respect the sovereignty of another person's mental space and recognize the potential consequences of our actions.

The Principle of Correspondence highlights that our inner world reflects the larger cosmos. Thus, mind reading could be seen as a means of understanding the broader patterns present in our existence. However, caution must be exercised to ensure that such knowledge is not misused.

The Principle of Cause and Effect emphasizes that every action has consequences, and mind reading is no exception. The intentions and mindfulness of the person engaging in the practice play a vital role in determining the ethicality of their actions. Utilizing occult knowledge with positive intentions and a clear understanding of the potential effects can help maintain a balance between respecting free will and harnessing occult power.

In conclusion, reading thoughts presents significant ethical implications and limitations. By assessing The Kybalion's three principles in conjunction with the stages of spiritual alchemy, we can understand the importance of intention and mindfulness in maintaining a balance between respecting free will and utilizing occult knowledge. The key themes that emerge from this analysis are the value of respecting others' mental sovereignty, the interconnectedness of personal and cosmic realities, and the necessity of mindfulness and positive intentions when engaging in any form of esoteric practice.
