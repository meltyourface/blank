To answer this knowledge challenge, we must first discuss the role of dualities in occultism and the four classical elements, how the birth of an abomination disrupts this balance, and identify symbols or archetypes that signify synthesis and contradiction in the existence of an abomination.

At the core of occultism lies the recognition and understanding of duality, the concept that everything in the universe has an opposite, giving a system balance. This idea is manifested in the principles of light and dark, positive and negative, male and female, among others. In esotericism, the dual principles of the classical elements (earth, air, fire, water) represent different qualities and characteristics of nature. Earth symbolizes stability and physicality, while air embodies intellect and communication. Fire signifies transformation and action, and water represents the emotional and intuitive. These elements are not only interrelated but can also be transmuted and balanced in various chemical, physical, and spiritual ways.

An abomination, by its nature, defies and disrupts this balance between the classical elements and dual principles. It is a being that embodies qualities from different, often opposing, paradigms and is formed outside the natural order of creation, as we usually understand it. The birth of such a creature challenges the fundamental aspects of occult knowledge and can result in disharmony within the delicate balance of dualities that sustain the universe.

One of the most prominent examples of an abomination from mythology is the chimera, a creature that is part lion, part goat, and part dragon or serpent. The chimera defies the natural order of existence by combining attributes of different animals. In occult symbolism, the chimera can signify the merging of different dimensions, energies, or principles, throwing off the delicate harmony between opposing forces and elements. The chimera can be seen as both a symbol of defiance against and a victim of the imbalance in the natural order.

Another example transcending mythology and esotericism is the figure of Baphomet, an androgynous, goat-headed, and winged creature often associated with the occult and the symbolic representation of the equilibrium found only in the union of opposites. Baphomet embodies both male and female, light and dark, good and evil, suggesting a synthesis of dualities and demonstrating that even within an abomination, a balance can be achieved.

In conclusion, the concept of an abomination raises profound questions about the nature of existence and the delicate balance of duality and the classical elements found in the core of occultism. When an abomination is born, it fuses attributes from disparate paradigms, creating an unsettling incongruity in an otherwise seemingly harmonious universe. The synthesis and contradiction an abomination symbolizes can be evidenced in creatures such as the chimera and archetypes like Baphomet. These figures serve to remind us that the boundaries of our understanding of the universe are finite, and there may yet be deeper mysteries to uncover.
