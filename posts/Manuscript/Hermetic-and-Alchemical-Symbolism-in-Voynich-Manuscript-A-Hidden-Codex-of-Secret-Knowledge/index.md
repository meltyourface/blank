Hermeticism is an esoteric philosophical and religious tradition that combines aspects of Greek philosophy, Gnostic thought, and Ancient Egyptian religion. Central to Hermeticism is the concept of the 'as above, so below' principle, illustrating the interconnectedness of the macrocosmic and microcosmic realms. Alchemy, on the other hand, is an ancient philosophical and proto-scientific tradition that aimed to transform base matter into more refined substances, such as gold or silver, and achieve a spiritual transformation and enlightenment.

Considering the intricate illustrations in the Voynich Manuscript, one can observe the symbolism interwoven with these esoteric concepts. The botanical illustrations could signify the base matter that Alchemists sought to transform, while the astrological diagrams may represent the macrocosmic realm that influences the microcosmic. The way in which these illustrations are presented in the manuscript may also be a reflection of the Hermetic principle of 'as above, so below.'

Additionally, the undeciphered script suggests that the knowledge contained within the manuscript is hidden, much like the secret knowledge sought after by practitioners of Hermeticism and Alchemy.
