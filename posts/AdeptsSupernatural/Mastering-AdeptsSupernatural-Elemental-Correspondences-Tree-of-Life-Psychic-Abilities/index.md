The Tree of Life, an archetypical symbol found in the Kabbalistic teachings of Jewish mysticism and the Hermetic Qabalah, depicts the divine structure of the universe. Comprised of ten spheres (Sephiroth) connected by twenty-two paths, the Tree of Life embodies the different aspects of God or the universe in its entirety. Each Sephira symbolizes aspects of human nature and the stages of spiritual progress. As an adept journeys through the Tree of Life, traversing from one Sephira to another, they gain insights into these universal aspects, allowing them to integrate them within themselves. This transformative journey fosters understanding, personal growth, and self-actualization.
