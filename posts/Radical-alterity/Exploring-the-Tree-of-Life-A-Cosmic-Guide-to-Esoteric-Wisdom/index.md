The Tree of Life comprises ten interconnected sephirot, or spheres, each representing an aspect of the divine. The sephirot are the emanations through which the unmanifested, infinite reality, often referred to as Ain Soph Aur in Kabbalistic tradition, becomes progressively refined and manifested into material reality. These emanations are organized into three pillars: the Pillar of Mercy, the Pillar of Severity, and the Pillar of Balance.

The sephirot delineate a path of spiritual self-discovery, allowing individuals to traverse from the mundane realm of everyday life to the boundless mysteries of the divine. In these traditions, the Tree of Life is a central guide in the aspirant's pursuit of gnosis, or direct knowledge of the divine, by providing a symbolic representation of the spiritual ascent from the depths of material existence to the heights of the divine realms.

In contemporary occult practice, the Tree of Life continues to be revered for its profound symbolism and ability to decipher esoteric knowledge. Modern interpretations of this ancient symbol, such as those found in Thelema and Chaos Magick, emphasize the inherent power of the individual to manifest their will in the world, defining the process not only as a spiritual ascent but as an ongoing reintegration of the various aspects of the self.
