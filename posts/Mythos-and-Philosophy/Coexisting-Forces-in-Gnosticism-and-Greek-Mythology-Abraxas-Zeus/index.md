To begin unraveling the challenge before us, we must first provide a brief background of the two mythos that this question concerns: Abraxas from Gnostic belief systems, and Zeus from Greek mythology. Once we establish a core understanding of their respective backgrounds and ideologies, we can delve further into exploring how these two seemingly contradictory forces coexist in the pursuit of providing profound insights on human nature, dualistic cosmology, and the path to enlightenment.

Abraxas, in Gnostic tradition, represents a higher power or god-like figure that is associated with the concept of the unification of opposites, incorporating both good and evil in its divine nature. As an ancient deity, it transcends both chaos and order, symbolizing the totality of existence. In Gnostic belief, Abraxas also represents the driving force behind reality, affecting both the physical and spiritual realms.

Zeus, on the other hand, is a prominent figure in Greek mythology. He is considered the king of the gods and the ruler of Mount Olympus. Zeus is often depicted as a just and righteous ruler, maintaining order and stability within the cosmos. His rule, however, is not without its tyrannical aspects, as he often seeks to suppress dissent and chaos in the pursuit of order.

Now that we have a foundational understanding of Abraxas and Zeus, let us investigate how these two forces may be reconciled within the broader context of Gnostic and Greek philosophical thought.

Both Gnostic and Greek philosophy present dualistic outlooks, wherein polar forces define reality, including the concepts of chaos and order, good and evil, and darkness and light. Gnostic thought encourages transcendence and unity while acknowledging the importance of these dual forces. In this sense, it offers a way to encompass the roles and symbolic properties of both Abraxas and Zeus.

To synthesize the ideas of Abraxas and Zeus, we must look to the idea of a dualistic cosmology which acknowledges the coexistence of their respective natures. While Zeus represents the order and structure that is necessary for society's function, Abraxas embodies the creative chaos that drives the essence of existence. By synthesizing these two forces, we reveal a profound truth: that chaos and order are inherently intertwined and necessary for the progression of life and the development of our spirit and consciousness.

In relation to human nature, the synthesis of Abraxas and Zeus emphasizes the importance of accepting and embracing both our rational, structured side and our chaotic, creative side. Through embracing both aspects, we can attain a greater understanding of ourselves and contribute to the ongoing interplay of chaos and order within the cosmos.

Finally, within the context of the path to enlightenment, the mythos of Abraxas and Zeus encourages us to strive for a balance between the opposing forces within ourselves and the world around us. Through embracing dualism and mastering the eternal dance of chaos and order, we can gain insight into the nature of existence and, ultimately, progress towards enlightenment.

In summary:
1. Abraxas and Zeus represent seemingly opposing forces in their respective mythos - Abraxas encompassing both chaos and order, and Zeus symbolizing order and control.
2. The key themes in reconciling these two opposing entities are the exploration of dualistic cosmology, human nature, and the path to enlightenment.
3. By synthesizing Abraxas and Zeus, we reveal the profound truth that chaos and order are necessary and interconnected aspects of existence.
