Alchemical texts are ancient manuscripts outlining the process of transmutation and the interplay of various natural elements. They employ a complex system of symbols to represent chemical elements, processes, and the transformation of substances. Kubler's "Hermetic Alchemy" classifies alchemical symbology into material symbols (e.g., metals, salts), process symbols (e.g., distillation, calcination), and objects with esoteric significance (e.g., alchemical egg, philosopher's stone).

Kabbalistic teachings stem from Jewish mysticism and mainly revolve around the study of the Tree of Life, itself a symbolic structure composed of ten interconnected spheres (sephiroth) representing different aspects of creation. Each sephira corresponds to a divine attribute, a Hebrew letter, an angelic order, a planet, and so on. The sephiroth are joined by 22 paths that embody the relationships that exist between the spheres.

The Hermetic principles are a collection of seven fundamental maxims attributed to the mythical figure Hermes Trismegistus – the purported author of the Corpus Hermeticum. These principles – Mentalism, Correspondence, Vibration, Polarity, Rhythm, Cause and Effect, and Gender – embody the ancient understanding of the cosmos and the nature of existence, encompassing both the material and immaterial aspects of reality.

Both concepts convey the idea that the microcosm reflects the macrocosm.

2. **Transformation**: Alchemical transmutation symbolizes the spiritual transformation of the individual. The Kabbalistic Tree of Life illustrates the process by which divine energy descends from the highest sphere (Kether) to the lowest (Malkuth) and then ascends back. This movement is mirrored in the Hermetic principle of Mentalism ("The All is Mind"), suggesting that our reality springs from divine consciousness and returns to it as we refine our spiritual essence.

Alchemical symbols represent elements and processes in the material world, while the Kabbalistic glyphs embody archetypal qualities of the divine. The Hermetic axioms contain concealed wisdom, symbolized in the caduceus of Hermes, a symbol of spiritual guidance and transformation.
