In order to provide a comprehensive response to the connection between angelic orders and their corresponding divine emanations through the lens of Dionysius the Areopagite's Celestial Hierarchy, and how this influences human spiritual ascent, we need to explore the following key aspects:

1. The Celestial Hierarchy's structure and the roles of various angelic orders.
2. The concept of divine emanation as seen in Dionysius' writings.
3. The process of human spiritual ascent in relation to the angelic intermediaries, messengers, and protective spirits.

Firstly, Dionysius the Areopagite's Celestial Hierarchy is a seminal work on the angelic realm, which delineates the structure and functions of angels into a hierarchical order. The hierarchy is divided into three main spheres, each consisting of three orders. The first sphere comprises Seraphim, Cherubim, and Thrones; the second sphere is made up of Dominions, Virtues, and Powers; and the final sphere consists of Principalities, Archangels, and Angels.

Within this structure, each angelic order possesses unique functions that correspond to specific aspects of God's divine emanations. For instance, the Seraphim are primarily associated with divine love, the Cherubim with divine wisdom, and the Thrones as the bearers of divine justice. This hierarchical arrangement embodies the principle of divine emanation, where the higher orders of angels are more immediately connected with the transcendent perfection of God, while lower orders have a more direct connection to the material universe.

This brings us to the second aspect: divine emanation. Divine emanation is a concept that stems from Neoplatonic philosophy, which posits the existence of a single source of being (the One) that continuously flows or emanates into creation. In the context of Dionysius' celestial hierarchy, divine emanation refers to the way in which God's attributes, presence, and energies are manifested in the created order through angelic intermediaries. The angelic hierarchy, therefore, acts as a channel or conduit through which divine emanation flows from the transcendent realm down to the level of humanity.

Concerning the human spiritual ascent, the angelic orders' functions as intermediaries, messengers, and protective spirits serve to facilitate humanity's progressive advancement towards a more profound communion with the divine. As individuals embark on their spiritual journey, they can appeal to the angelic realm for guidance, protection, and assistance in various ways.

The protective aspects of the angelic orders involve safeguarding humans from threats, both spiritual and material. For example, the virtues may intervene to help individuals resist temptation and perform acts of courage, while principalities ensure general harmony between human authority and the divine order. As messengers, the Archangels transmit God's divine will to mankind, imparting knowledge and wisdom so that they may grow spiritually.

In essence, the angels' intermediary role provides a bridge between humanity and the transcendent realm, helping individuals to access divine grace and guidance as they endeavor to ascend the spiritual ladder and ultimately achieve a state of unification with God.

In conclusion, Dionysius the Areopagite's Celestial Hierarchy presents a complex relationship between angelic orders and their corresponding divine emanations, which is essential for understanding the means of human spiritual ascent. The hierarchical structure of angelic orders represents the Neoplatonic principle of divine emanation, illustrating the progressive outflow of God's divine attributes throughout creation. Consequently, the angels serve as intermediaries, messengers, and protective spirits, supporting and guiding individuals on their spiritual journey towards communion with the divine.
