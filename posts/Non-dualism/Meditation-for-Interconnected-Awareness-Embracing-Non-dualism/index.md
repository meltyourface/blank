Begin by finding a quiet, comfortable space where you can sit or lie down. Close your eyes and start to relax your body from head to toe. Take several deep breaths, inhaling through your nose and exhaling through your mouth. Let each inhale bring awareness to your body and each exhale release tension and stress.

Now, begin to focus on your breath. Imagine your breath is a golden thread, connecting you to the present moment, as well as the ancient wisdom of Non-dualist teachings such as Advaita Vedanta and Dzogchen. Follow the rhythm of your breath as the thread moves, woven into the very fabric of existence, reminding you of the interconnectedness of all things.

As you breathe, you might notice thoughts or emotions begin to arise. Recognize them without judgment, and allow them to pass by, returning your focus to the golden thread of breath. This mindfulness technique will help you to cultivate a relaxed and focused awareness of the present moment.

Gently shift your focus to the concept of interconnectedness. Visualize each breath weaving a vast tapestry of creation, seamlessly merging with the breaths of all beings, past, present, and future. This tapestry reveals the truth of Non-dualism â€“ the ultimate oneness and unity of all existence.

Feel the connection between yourself and all living things, recognizing the illusory nature of separation. Contemplate the ancient Advaita Vedanta metaphor of Indra's Net, in which each jewel reflects every other jewel in an infinitely interconnected web; just as each being reflects the true nature of the entire universe.

Introduce subtle auditory elements by honing in on the sounds in your environment, whether it's the rustle of leaves outside, the hum of an air conditioner, or even the beating of your heart. Bring awareness to these sounds as expressions of the interconnectedness between yourself and the world around you.

In Dzogchen, awareness is considered to be the natural, primordial state of existence. You will inevitably encounter challenges and misconceptions along the path to awakening, such as feelings of distraction or doubt about your progress. When these arise, simply recognize them as part of your experience and return your focus to breath awareness and the interconnectedness of all things.

As your meditation continues, visualize your self-identity dissolving like a drop of water into the ocean, leaving only expansive, limitless awareness. Embrace the truth that there is no distinction between self and others, and that everything is connected in one unified, divine essence.

Now, slowly begin to bring your attention back to your body. Become aware of your surroundings, the feeling of your body touching the surface beneath you, and the gentle rhythm of your breath. Slowly open your eyes, maintaining the deep sense of interconnectedness and oneness you experienced during your meditation.

Take a few moments to sit with this experience, reflecting on your newfound understanding of Non-dualism. Compose yourself and, when you feel ready, carry with you the wisdom of Non-dualism and interconnectedness throughout the rest of your day. Remember that each breath you take is part of the eternal dance of existence, and you are an essential note in the symphony of life.
