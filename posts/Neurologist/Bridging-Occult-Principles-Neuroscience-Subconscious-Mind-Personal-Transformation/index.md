  Answer:
  
  To address this challenge, we must first provide a foundational understanding of the key concepts involved: the neurological functions of the human brain, the subconscious mind, occult teachings, and modern neuroscience. Once these are defined and contextualized, we will explore their connections and conclude with a summary of the key themes, ideas, and conclusions.
  
  1. Neurological Functions of the Human Brain:
  The human brain is a complex organ responsible for the processing and transmission of information. It contains approximately 100 billion neurons, or nerve cells, which are interconnected and communicate through synaptic connections. There are three primary components that play critical roles in neurological functions: the cerebrum, responsible for higher cognitive functions such as thinking and memory; the cerebellum, which controls motor functions, balance, and coordination; and the brainstem, responsible for regulating basic life functions like breathing and heartbeat.
  
  2. Subconscious Mind:
  The subconscious mind refers to the portion of the brain that operates below the level of conscious awareness, controlling a variety of functions, including habits, beliefs, instincts, automatic reactions, and emotions. Notably, the subconscious mind operates more quickly and processes more information than the conscious mind, serving as a repository for memories and experiences.
  
  3. Occult Teachings:
  Occult teachings encompass a diverse range of beliefs and practices concerned with the study and understanding of hidden or esoteric knowledge, often delving into the realm of the spiritual, mystical, and magical. The teachings often focus on understanding the self, the universe, and the relationships between the two.
  
  4. Modern Neuroscience:
  Modern neuroscience is the scientific study of the nervous system, which seeks to understand the neurological and cognitive processes that underlie human behavior, emotion, and thought. Neuroscience incorporates various disciplines, such as biology, psychology, computer science, and medicine.
  
  Now that we have established the foundational concepts, we can explore the intersection between the neurological functions of the human brain and the subconscious mind, drawing from both occult teachings and modern neuroscience.
  
  Occult teachings, particularly in traditions such as Qabalah, Hermeticism, and Tantra, often emphasize the importance of the subconscious mind and its role in shaping our realities. One fundamental belief in these teachings is that the subconscious mind, when influenced and aligned with conscious intention, can serve as a vital tool in achieving personal transformation and spiritual growth.
  
  Modern neuroscience has contributed to our understanding of the subconscious mind by revealing the neurobiological processes underlying it. For example, neuroscientists have identified specific regions in the brain, such as the limbic system and the reticular activating system, that are involved in the regulation and processing of subconscious information.
  
  Neuroplasticity, or the ability of the brain to change and adapt in response to experiences, also demonstrates the potential for the subconscious mind to evolve and develop. Occult practices, such as meditation, visualization, and affirmative rituals, are designed to tap into the subconscious mind and facilitate transformative change by creating new neural pathways, thus providing a bridge between the esoteric principles and their neurological basis.
  
  Furthermore, research on altered states of consciousness, such as those attained through meditation or the use of entheogens, has provided insights into the mechanisms through which the subconscious mind can be accessed and integrated with the conscious mind. These altered states may allow for a more profound interaction between the two, expanding conscious awareness and enabling personal growth.
  
  In conclusion, the principles of the neurological functions of the human brain can help us better understand the subconscious mind by illuminating the underlying neurobiological mechanisms involved. Both occult teachings and modern neuroscience contribute valuable insights in this domain, highlighting the potential for personal transformation through the alignment and integration of the subconscious and conscious minds. Key themes include the role of the subconscious mind in shaping our realities, the neurobiological roots of esoteric practices, and the potential for neuroplasticity in facilitating change and growth.
