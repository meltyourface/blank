To address this knowledge challenge, we shall explore the dynamics of occultism and esotericism in addressing transformation and personal growth. The process incorporates the shadow self, duality, and integration of the light and darkness in constructing a personal affirmation of life.

First, let's unpack the concept of the shadow self in the context of the esoteric and occult. Pioneered by Swiss psychiatrist Carl Jung, the shadow is the aspect of ourselves that is unseen, repressed, and often denied, consisting of desires, emotions, and instincts that may not align with our mainstream identity. The presence of the shadow in esoteric thought is significant because it embodies the dual nature of existence. Both light and darkness are integral elements of the soul, and spiritual growth hinges on harmonizing these polarities rather than leaning solely into positivity. 

Embracing our shadow self requires profound honesty and courage. To integrate our darker aspects, we must accept them as valuable contributions towards our identity. A personal affirmation of life that authentically embraces both light and darkness would thus involve accepting the whole of our experience – the joy and the suffering, the love, and the disappointments – as an essential part of our spiritual journey.

Engaging with our shadow self can transpire through journaling, dream work, meditation, and other reflective practices. By acknowledging and expressing our inner darkness, we can achieve a deeper understanding of ourselves, ultimately catalyzing growth and self-expression. Through this exploration, we foster emotional intelligence and resilience – qualities that serve our personal development and strengthen our relationships with others.

Drawing from the Hermetic principle of "As above, so below," our inner transformation ripples outward, affecting the collective human experience. When we reconcile our dual nature, we become capable of serving humanity from a more grounded and holistic perspective. Our ability to empathize, guide, and support others stems not only from basking in life's light but by embracing the teachings hidden in our darkness.

To exemplify this concept, consider the metaphor of a tree. The tree's roots extend into the darkness of the earth, an essential and inseparable part of the same organism that reaches towards the light. Both aspects — the darkness below and the light above — contribute to the tree's vitality and growth, each containing wisdom and experience. By embracing these dualities, the tree thrives, symbolizing how we can remain rooted in our darker aspects while still seeking light and positive development.

In conclusion, to construct a personal affirmation of life that authentically integrates both light and darkness, we must embrace and engage with our shadow selves. By accepting and reconciling our dual nature, we catalyze our growth, self-expression, and deep understanding. In doing so, we not only foster our personal development but contribute to the collective understanding and enrichment of the human experience. The tree metaphor, with its roots grounded in darkness and branches reaching towards light, encapsulates the wisdom of integrating the paradox of duality in our spiritual journey. The power of transformation lies in harmonizing our light and darkness, living in wholeness, and fostering a more profound connection with ourselves and others.
