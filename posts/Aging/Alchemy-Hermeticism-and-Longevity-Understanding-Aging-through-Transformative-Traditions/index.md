To respond to this knowledge challenge, let us first understand the individual concepts of alchemy, hermeticism, and longevity, the discovery of the Philosopher's Stone, and the growth and decline of the physical and mental aspects of an individual as they age:

1. ****Alchemy****: Alchemy is an ancient philosophical and proto-scientific tradition originating in Greco-Roman Egypt. It is primarily focused on the exploration and transformation of matter, particularly metals, and on the search for a universal elixir or panacea. This elixir, encompassed in the legendary Philosopher's Stone, was thought to grant longevity, immortality, or spiritual enlightenment.

2. ****Hermeticism****: Hermeticism is an esoteric tradition rooted in the teachings of Hermes Trismegistus, a legendary figure of Egyptian and Greek origin. Its core beliefs revolve around the pursuit of divine wisdom and understanding of the universe's interconnectedness through the study of astrology, alchemy, and theology. Hermeticism emphasizes the spiritual evolution of the individual through self-knowledge and the exploration of the inner realms of existence.

3. ****Longevity****: Longevity is the concept of extending one's lifespan and improving the quality of life as it progresses. In the context of alchemy and hermeticism, longevity refers to the quest for immortality or an extended life, attained through spiritual or physical means, such as the Philosopher's Stone.

Now that we have established a fundamental understanding of the concepts presented in the question, let us examine their interplay and relation to the Philosopher's Stone and aging.

The Philosopher's Stone lies at the junction of alchemy, hermeticism, and longevity. It was believed that the Stone could grant its possessor eternal wisdom, youth, and even immortality. Its attainment represented both the ultimate goal of the alchemical process and the culmination of the hermetic journey - the awakening of divine knowledge and the realization of one's true potential as a spiritual being.

As individuals age, they undergo a series of physical and cognitive changes, such as declining muscle mass, reduced cognitive function, and a higher susceptibility to disease. In our quest to comprehend and potentially overcome these challenges, we can consider the metaphysical implications of aging and the teachings of alchemy and hermeticism.

Viewing the aging process through the lens of alchemy, we find that it embodies the principles of transformation and transmutation. The aged person's body and mind undergo a series of gradual alterations, as their life force (represented by the alchemical concept of "prima materia") diminishes over time. Alchemists sought to reverse or even transcend this deterioration by achieving the Philosopher's Stone, symbolizing a transformation that transcends the limits of our mortal existence.

Hermeticism can shed light on the metaphysical aspects of aging as well. According to its tenets, the universe is an interconnected multilayered entity, in which microcosmic elements of human existence correspond to larger macrocosmic patterns. Through this lens, aging symbolizes the cyclical patterns of life, correlated to the waxing and waning of celestial bodies, the changing of seasons, or even the progression of political and social eras.

In conclusion, alchemy, hermeticism, and the quest for longevity are interconnected in the pursuit of understanding the nature of aging and life's cyclical patterns. The Philosopher's Stone, the most elusive goal in these traditions, represents the quintessence of human potential and the possibility of transcending the seemingly inevitable decline of our mortal existence. By appreciating these aspects, we can glean insights into the metaphysical dimensions of aging and our quest for self-knowledge, wisdom, and the harmonious interplay of life's forces.
