In twilight's fervent grasp, we dance,
Venus and Mars in fabled trance.
A secret garden, yon citadel's view,
Where sacred syllables form clandestine brew.

Her raven tresses veil the night,
Their silky strands guard Elysian delight.
Pandora's chest unveiled at last,
Aquiver, an archer draws back, steadfast.

Graze Cytherea's scribe, pen dipped in passion,
Ink on parchment, my love eternally fashioned.
Fingers trace whispers of ancient scrolls,
Irresistible glyphs, unearthing our souls.

Dionysian shades shroud fervent fire,
Quandary of cloaked desires take higher.
The chimera awaits, in twilight's den,
To unveil the Seraph's star, we begin.

Riddles writ in shadow and light,
Caress the mind's tongue, as Lipsmite.
Unravel the cipher, if thee may,
And taste the ambrosia of love's sweet play.
