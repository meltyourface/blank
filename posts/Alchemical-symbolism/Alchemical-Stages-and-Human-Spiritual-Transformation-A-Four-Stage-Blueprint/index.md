To address the knowledge challenge at hand, we first need to establish the foundational concepts in Hermetic philosophy, Jungian psychology, and alchemy. Then, we will elucidate the connections between the four primary alchemical stages, their symbolism, and their association with human spiritual transformation and the four classical elements. Finally, we will summarize the key themes, ideas, and conclusions drawn.

Foundational Concepts:

1. Hermetic philosophy: This is a philosophical tradition based on the teachings of Hermes Trismegistus, an ancient Egyptian priest and philosopher. It is often associated with concepts like theurgy, magic, and alchemy, as well as the notion of "As above, so below", which implies a deep interconnection between the spiritual and physical realms. 

2. Jungian psychology: The analytical psychology of Carl Gustav Jung, which emphasizes the role of unconscious processes, archetypes, and symbols in understanding both the psyche and the development of human beings. One of Jung's main contributions was the exploration of alchemical symbolism, linking it to human psychological transformation processes.

3. Alchemy: An ancient practice and spiritual belief system, which focuses on the idea of transmutation – changing base metals into gold, as well as the purification and transformation of the human soul. In alchemy, different stages of the process are symbolically linked to various elements and colors.

The Four Primary Alchemical Stages:

1. Nigredo (Blackness): This stage of alchemy represents the initial state of chaos, darkness, and disintegration. It involves a process of putrefaction and decomposition that allows for the release of hidden potential within matter or the human soul. In Jungian terms, it corresponds to the encounter with the Shadow archetype, which represents the unconscious, repressed aspects of the psyche. Symbolically, Nigredo connects with the element of Earth, as it is the dense and opaque substratum from which spiritual growth emerges.

2. Albedo (Whiteness): The second stage, Albedo, is characterized by purification, washing, and the emergence of clarity. It signifies the separation of elements, extraction of the Yin essence, and the integration of opposing forces within the psyche. In Jungian psychology, it resonates with the concept of individuation – the integration of unconscious content into consciousness, leading to psychological wholeness. Albedo is linked with the element of Water, denoting cleansing and emotional release.

3. Citrinitas (Yellowness): The Citrinitas stage represents the transformation of the purified essence acquired during Albedo into a higher form. Symbolically, it is the process of sublimation and the dawning of spiritual enlightenment in the alchemical context and the nurturing of individuation in the Jungian framework. This stage corresponds to the element of Air, signifying intellect, spiritual illumination, and the higher realms of consciousness.

4. Rubedo (Redness): The final stage in alchemy is Rubedo, marked by the unification of all previous materials and processes to create the Philosopher's Stone or Elixir of Life – a metaphor for attaining spiritual immortality, self-mastery, and ultimate wisdom. In Jungian terms, this means the achievement of psychic wholeness and integration of the Self archetype. Rubedo is associated with the element of Fire, which represents passion, transformation, and divine essence.

In summary, the four primary alchemical stages – Nigredo, Albedo, Citrinitas, and Rubedo – provide an allegorical framework for understanding the process of human spiritual transformation, echoing core principles of both Hermetic philosophy and Jungian psychology. The symbolic connections with the classical elements (Earth, Water, Air, and Fire) underscore the interconnectedness of these disciplines. The journey through these alchemical stages can be seen as a blueprint for personal growth and a deeper understanding of the universal patterns underlying the human experience.
