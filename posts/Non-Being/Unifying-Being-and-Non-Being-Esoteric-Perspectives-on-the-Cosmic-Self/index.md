Answering this knowledge challenge involves the exploration of various esoteric and occult principles that provide a framework for understanding how being and non-being can coexist within the context of unity, transcendental archetypes, and negation of dualism. We will also address the concept of the dissolution of self in this context.

To begin with, let us consider the idea of the unity of existence. The unity of existence is rooted in the fundamental principle of the interconnectedness of all things and the notion that everything in the cosmos is part of an all-encompassing whole. This is best encapsulated by the Hermetic principle "As above, so below; as within, so without." This principle teaches us that everything is connected to everything else, and that there is ultimately no separation between the various levels of existence, whether they be physical, mental, or spiritual.

Having established the premise of the unity of existence, let us now delve into the role of transcendental archetypes in this context. In the realm of the esoteric, archetypes are understood as universal symbolic templates that represent key aspects of the human experience. They are derived from the collective unconscious and shape the patterns of our thoughts, emotions, and perceptions. Examples of these archetypes can be found in the Major Arcana of the Tarot and the archetypal figures within mythology.

Now, the connection between transcendental archetypes and the unity of existence can be best understood through the idea of the World Soul or the Anima Mundi. This is a cosmic, all-pervading, animating presence that underlies and connects everything in existence. Transcendental archetypes, as innate aspects of the World Soul, are, therefore, inherent to the fabric of the universe and are expressions of the unity of existence.

Next, let's address the negation of dualism. In esoteric teachings, the ultimate reality is often described as being beyond duality and opposition. This is crucial for understanding non-being within the scope of occult teachings. The concept of non-dualism posits that seemingly opposing forces are actually interconnected and complementary aspects of the One. For example, the Chinese philosophy of Taoism emphasizes the interdependence of Yin and Yang, while the Hindu concept of Advaita Vedanta asserts the singular nature of Brahman, which transcends all illusionary dualities.

In the context of being and non-being, this non-dualistic framework allows us to perceive non-being not as an absence, void, or negation of existence, but as an integral and complementary aspect of the unified reality that encompasses both being and non-being. Thus, negation of dualism allows us to reconcile the paradoxical coexistence of being and non-being in the greater unity of existence.

Lastly, we must examine the dissolution of self. The dissolution of self can be thought of as the state in which the individual ego, the illusionary sense of separation from the All, is dissolved, allowing an individual to experience the innate unity of existence. This dissolution can be achieved through meditation, spiritual practices, or even spontaneous mystical experiences. By letting go of the illusion of separateness, individuals can transcend their limited perspective and embrace the unified reality where being and non-being are inseparably bound.

In conclusion, the seemingly paradoxical coexistence of being and non-being in the context of occult teachings can be reconciled by understanding the unity of existence, drawing connections between transcendental archetypes and the World Soul, negating dualism, and ultimately dissolving the sense of self. By embracing this framework, it becomes clear how being and non-being can coalesce into a single, unified reality beyond opposition and duality.
