To answer this knowledge challenge, let us first understand the key concepts and their interrelationships.

1. The Abyss as a liminal space to transcend in alchemical and initiatory processes: This refers to the idea of the Abyss as a transitional phase or threshold that an individual must cross to attain higher levels of spiritual growth and self-realization. In both alchemy and initiation rites, the Abyss signifies a metaphorical death and rebirth, a dissolution of the old self to give way to a new, transformed self.

2. Qliphoth: The Qliphoth are the adverse aspects or `shells' of the divine emanations (or Sephiroth) in the Kabbalistic Tree of Life. They represent the forces of chaos, destruction, and imbalance â€“ the dark counterparts that exist alongside the divine order.

3. Death card in Tarot: The Death card is often misunderstood as a physical death; however, it symbolizes transformation, change, and the end of cycles. It signifies the necessity of shedding the old to make way for the new. In the context of the Abyss, it represents the metaphorical death that the adept must encounter and surpass to evolve spiritually.

4. Psychological dissociative states in the dark night of the soul: The dark night of the soul is a concept popularized by the Spanish mystic St. John of the Cross. It refers to a phase of spiritual travail, during which the individual experiences intense despair, alienation, and desolation. The psychological dissociative states during a dark night of the soul can be akin to losing touch with one's ego, identity, and reality, leading to a space for rebirth and transformation.

Considering these key concepts, the challenge question that synthesizes all aspects is:

"How can the adept, confronted by the alchemical and initiatory trials of the Abyss, embrace the transformational power of the Qliphoth and the Death card, whilst navigating the psychological dissociation experienced during the dark night of the soul, to emerge reborn and spiritually transcended?"

The key themes, ideas, and conclusions to grasp from this question are:

1. The Abyss is a liminal space in which initiatory and alchemical processes happen, where dissolution of the old self and rebirth of the transformed self occur.
2. The Qliphoth represent the chaotic and destructive aspects of spiritual existence, which the individual must embrace to become transformed.
3. The Death card signifies transformative change and an end to old cycles, echoing the transformative power experienced in the Abyss.
4. During the dark night of the soul, the adept endures a state of psychological dissociation in which they have to grasp the rebirth and transformation that will emerge from it.

Overall, this question challenges individuals to understand and harness the destructive and chaotic forces within and around them while embracing their transformative potential. By doing so, the adept can successfully navigate the trials of the Abyss and emerge reborn and transcended, ready to fulfill their higher spiritual aspirations.
