To fully respond to this knowledge challenge, we need to first understand the key concepts involved in the process of spiritual transcendence, the nature of unease, the significance of societal, cosmic, and personal symbols, and how they can be harnessed for intellectual analysis and synthesis leading to self-awareness and transformation.

Spiritual transcendence refers to the process of evolving beyond one's ordinary state of consciousness and embracing a heightened sense of connection with the universe, the self, and others. This involves transcending the perception of reality as purely physical and delving into the underlying spiritual energies or vibrations.

Unease, in the context of spiritual transcendence, can be defined as a sense of disquiet or discomfort that pushes the adept towards exploring and embracing the unknown. The power of unease can be used as a catalyst for evolution, encouraging the seeker to confront limiting beliefs and emotions.

Societal, cosmic, and personal symbols are interconnected, and they play a crucial role in the adept's journey. Societal symbols link to shared experiences and values within a particular culture, while cosmic symbols represent universal patterns and archetypes across time and space. Personal symbols, on the other hand, are unique to the individual and encompass their experiences, memories, and emotions. These three types of symbols converge to influence our perceptions and understanding of reality.

Now, to address the challenge of harnessing the power of unease and utilizing the convergence of symbols to effectively analyze and synthesize multidimensional insights, we can detail the following steps:

1. **Acknowledge unease**: Recognize the uneasy feeling as an invitation to delve deeper into one's consciousness and examine the origins of the unease. This might involve exploring the societal, cosmic, and personal symbols that are evoking such discomfort.

2. **Investigate the symbols**: Unravel the meaning and context behind each symbol, and seek to understand how they interact with each other. Reflect on how these symbols have shaped one's beliefs and perceptions and identify any recurring patterns or themes.

3. **Analyze the insights**: Identify the insights that emerge from examining these symbols and seek connections between these insights and one's current state of awareness. This involves critically evaluating one's beliefs, assumptions, and emotions.

4. **Synthesize the insights**: Integrate the newfound understanding into a cohesive framework or narrative that provides a holistic understanding of the situation. This involves weaving together the various strands of knowledge and experience to create a coherent picture of reality and the self.

5. **Apply the insights**: Use the synthesized information for practical purposes by adapting one's behavior, thoughts, and emotional responses to align more closely with the newfound understanding. This might involve taking specific actions, adopting new habits, or transforming one's personal worldview.

6. **Reflect on the transformation**: Observe and appreciate the transformation that has occurred within oneself and throughout one's life as a result of harnessing the power of unease and working with the convergence of symbols. Recognize that this process is ongoing and that new insights and growth opportunities will continue to arise.

In summary, spiritual transcendence can be achieved through harnessing the power of unease stemming from the convergence of societal, cosmic, and personal symbols. By acknowledging the unease, investigating its origins in the converging symbols, analyzing and synthesizing the insights garnered, applying them to one's life, and reflecting on the ensuing transformation, the adept can effectively analyze and synthesize multidimensional insights. This process ultimately leads to heightened self-awareness and transformation, as the individual continues to evolve and transcend limiting beliefs and emotions.
