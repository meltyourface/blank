### Chapter 3: Historical Aspects of the Female Gaze in the Occult

Throughout history, women have played crucial roles in the realm of the occult, despite the gender imbalance often inherent in our understanding of these practices. Female practitioners, mystics, and oracles have contributed to the development and sustaining of various esoteric traditions. **Key historical figures include**:

1. Circe – A sorceress from Greek mythology who was proficient in potions, herbalism, and shape-shifting.
2. Marie Laveau – Known as the Voodoo Queen of New Orleans, she was a renowned practitioner of Voodoo and a powerful spiritual leader.
3. Hildegard von Bingen – A Christian mystic and author, known for her visions and extensive contributions to theology, science, and the arts.
