To respond to this knowledge challenge, I will create a unique transliterated liturgical composition that draws upon comparative symbolism from three distinct esoteric traditions – namely Kabbalah, Hermeticism, and Hinduism. The composition will reflect the interconnectivity of human consciousness with the divine purpose and portray the significance of such a connection.

**Title**: The Sacred Nexus

Verse 1: (Kabbalah Symbolism)
From the roots of the sacred Tree of Life, Ein Sof (Infinite),
Where the ten spheres known as Sefirot unite,
Emanates the divine, unspeakable name of YHVH,
A blueprint from which all reality is shaped.

In the realm of Atziluth, pure essence divine,
The supernal triad, Kether, Chokhmah, and Binah combine,
Symbolizing the unity of human thought and divine will,
Where knowledge grows and our destiny fulfilled.

**Chorus**: (Unifying Theme – Interconnectivity of Human Consciousness and Divine Purpose)
The divine within us, in every breath and beat,
Our consciousness branching like the Tree of Life complete,
We seek the sacred nexus, seekers of truth untold,
As one, with the cosmos, the mysteries we unfold.

Verse 2: (Hermeticism Symbolism)
From the famed Emerald Tablet of Hermes Trismegistus,
"As above, so below; as within, so without," written thus.
At the heart lie the seven principles within the Kybalion,
A sacred wisdom to guide us beyond the profane frisson.

Vibration and correspondence, polarity and rhythm,
Tied through the universal principle of mentalism,
Understanding our place in the cosmic plan,
As mirrors of the divine, God's human spawn.

**Chorus**:
The divine within us, in every breath and beat,
Our consciousness branching like the Tree of Life complete,
We seek the sacred nexus, seekers of truth untold,
As one, with the cosmos, the mysteries we unfold.

Verse 3: (Hinduism Symbolism)
Within the vast ocean of Sanatan Dharma,
Lies the wisdom residing in AUM, the sacred mantra.
Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva form the Holy Triad,
Representing creation, preservation, and dissolution – each a divine comrade.

From the Bhagavad Gita arise the concepts of Atman and Dharma,
Unfurling the cosmic order in the heart of every Karma.
The path of Yoga, showing union divine,
Between human consciousness and the infinite divine line.

**Chorus**:
The divine within us, in every breath and beat,
Our consciousness branching like the Tree of Life complete,
We seek the sacred nexus, seekers of truth untold,
As one, with the cosmos, the mysteries we unfold.

Essential themes and concepts include:

1. The Tree of Life from Kabbalah symbolizing the divine blueprint and interconnectivity of all things.
2. The principles from the Hermetic tradition, particularly the concept of 'As above, so below,' highlighting the interconnected nature of existence.
3. The Hindu concepts of Dharma and Yoga, which emphasize the importance of embracing one's purpose and unity with the Divine.
4. The unifying theme - The interconnectivity of human consciousness and the divine purpose, exemplified through the chorus.

Together, these themes and concepts create a coherent and profound composition reflecting the human quest for understanding our place within the cosmos and the interconnected nature of human consciousness with divine purpose.
