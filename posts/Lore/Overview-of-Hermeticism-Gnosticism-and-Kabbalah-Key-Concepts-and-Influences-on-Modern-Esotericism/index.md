To address this knowledge challenge, I will first provide individual overviews of Hermeticism, Gnosticism, and Kabbalah. I will then highlight the overarching principles and mutual intricacies, followed by an exploration of their intersections and how this has influenced the development of modern esoteric thought and practices.

1. **Hermeticism**:
Hermeticism is an esoteric philosophical and spiritual tradition based primarily on the writings attributed to Hermes Trismegistus ("Thrice Great"). Its core texts, known collectively as the Hermetica, emerged in the Roman Empire, around the 2nd and 3rd centuries CE. The primary themes of Hermeticism include the unity of existence, the concept of an inner divine spark in each person, and the idea that knowledge of the divine and the cosmos can lead to spiritual ascension.

2. **Gnosticism**:
Gnosticism is a diverse collection of religious and philosophical movements that originated in the 1st and 2nd centuries CE. Gnostic teachings emphasize the belief in a transcendent, unknowable God, the existence of a lower deity or "Demiurge" responsible for the flawed material world, and the idea that salvation comes through attaining gnosis, or direct knowledge of the divine mysteries. Gnosticism encompasses various expressions, but one of the key hallmarks is its dualistic worldview, which pits spiritual knowledge against material ignorance.

3. **Kabbalah**:
Kabbalah is a form of Jewish mysticism that originated between the 12th and 13th centuries CE in Provence, France. Its central text, the Zohar, explores the hidden meanings and structures of the Hebrew Scriptures, particularly the Torah. Kabbalah posits that the divine essence manifests through ten emanations, or sefirot, in a structure known as the Tree of Life. Its practices focus on meditation, contemplation, and the manipulation of these divine energies to elevate the soul and restore harmony with the divine.

Now, let's identify the overarching principles and mutual intricacies of these three esoteric systems:

1. **Hidden knowledge and divine revelation**:
All three traditions emphasize the importance of acquiring hidden, esoteric knowledge and direct divine revelation. Hermeticism involves the attainment of divine wisdom, Gnosticism centers on gnosis, and Kabbalistic practices aim to gain a deeper understanding of the divine mysteries.

2. **Emphasis on self-transformation**:
The pursuit of knowledge and direct divine experience in these traditions is not purely intellectual. It also involves personal transformation, with the aim of spiritual ascension, self-realization and ultimately, the union with the divine.

3. **Dualistic or complementary views on reality**:
These spiritual systems often present a dualistic or complementary view of reality, where higher spiritual realms contrast with the imperfect material world. In Gnosticism, the Demiurge maintains the material world, while Hermeticism calls for uniting the duality of spirit and matter.

Now, let's explore the intersections and influences on modern esoteric thought and practices:

1. **Influence on alchemy and magic**: 
Hermeticism, Gnosticism, and Kabbalah each have influenced the development of Western esoteric magical traditions and alchemy, particularly the quest to transmute base metals into gold or to attain immortality, representing spiritual transformation.

2. **Syncretism and syntheses**:
Modern esotericism is influenced by the syncretism found in all three traditions. As different expressions of spiritual truth, they are often seen as complementary, mutually enriching, or forming a broader universal perspective. Theosophy, for example, draws on these traditions to create a comprehensive spiritual system.

3. **Prominence in contemporary esoteric groups**:
All three systems enjoy continued relevance in contemporary esoteric and occult societies, such as the Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn, the Ordo Templi Orientis, and various Gnostic and Kabbalistic orders.

To summarize, Hermeticism, Gnosticism, and Kabbalah share several core principles, including the pursuit of hidden knowledge, self-transformation, and viewing reality through dualistic or complementary lenses. Their intersections have significantly influenced the development of modern esoteric thought and practice, with each tradition contributing unique perspectives and providing a foundation for syncretism and synthesis in contemporary esotericism.
