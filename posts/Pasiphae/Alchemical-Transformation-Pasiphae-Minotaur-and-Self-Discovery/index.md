The myth of Pasiphae and the Minotaur originates from a pre-Hellenic, Minoan context, which later became part of the Greek mythological and esoteric tradition. Pasiphae, a queen of Crete, was the wife of King Minos and a daughter of the Sun god, Helios. When Minos dishonored Poseidon, the sea god punished him by causing Pasiphae to fall in love with a bull, resulting in the birth of the Minotaur, half man and half bull. This creature was imprisoned in a labyrinth until it was killed by the Athenian hero Theseus.
