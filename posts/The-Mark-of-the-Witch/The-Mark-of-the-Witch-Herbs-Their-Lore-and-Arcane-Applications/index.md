## I. Introduction
The following is a comprehensive catalog of herbs specifically utilized within The Mark of the Witch domain, detailing their corresponding mystical attributes, historical associations with prominent witches, and distinct applications in various arcane rituals and spells. This knowledge is vital for the practitioning witch, as these plants and herbs have been imbued with potent energies and have immense transformative capacities. 

### II. Catalog of Herbs

1. Belladonna (Atropa belladonna)
Mystical Attributes: Enhanced psychic powers, divination, prophetic dreams
Historical Associations: Belladonna, also known as deadly nightshade, has long been associated with the reputed witch Circe from Greek mythology. She was known to use it for casting spells and enchanting potions.
Arcane Rituals and Spells: Herb used in flying ointments, promotes astral projection, and deepens meditation.

2. Mugwort (Artemisia vulgaris)
Mystical Attributes: Protection, divination, clairvoyance
Historical Associations: Mugwort has strong ties to the goddess Artemis, who was believed to protect women and facilitate divination. Witches throughout history have used it for psychic work and enhancing their intuitive abilities.
Arcane Rituals and Spells: Mugwort is used for smudging, inducing prophetic dreams, and for strengthening spells of protection.

3. Mandrake (Mandragora officinarum)
Mystical Attributes: Fertility, protection, and attraction
Historical Associations: Mandrake has a long history with witchcraft and folklore, being a plant with anthropomorphic roots. It has been associated with the biblical story of Rachel and Leah, as well as the witch of the medieval period, who often used it for love spells.
Arcane Rituals and Spells: Used in attracting love, bringing good fortune, and protecting against malevolent spirits.

4. Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium)
Mystical Attributes: Psychic powers, transformation, summoning
Historical Associations: Mythically connected to the Egyptian goddess Isis, wormwood has deep connections to the realm of spirits. Witches and practitioners have employed it for divination and communication with the dead.
Arcane Rituals and Spells: Used in summoning spirits, making magical inks, and enhancing astral travel.

5. Yarrow (Achillea millefolium)
Mystical Attributes: Healing, protection, clarity
Historical Associations: Yarrow has long been associated with the ancient Greek hero Achilles, who used it for its healing properties. Witches traditionally considered yarrow as a powerful herb for protection and maintaining clarity during spellwork.
Arcane Rituals and Spells: Employed in healing rituals, divination, and spells to repel negative energies.

### III. Conclusion
Understanding the properties and historical associations of these herbs allows the practitioning witch to effectively harness their inherent power. The Mark of the Witch domain benefits tremendously from the natural synergies amongst these plants, and their applications in various arcane rituals and spells have the potential to profoundly transform the experiences and outcomes of those who wield them. It is essential that any witch seeks deeper knowledge about these magical herbs to advance their craft and unlock their full potential.
