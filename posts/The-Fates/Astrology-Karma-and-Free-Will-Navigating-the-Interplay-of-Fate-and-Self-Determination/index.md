To understand the interplay between astrology, karmic cycles, and the concept of free will, we first need to analyze the individual concepts and their historical connections to The Fates and the wider occult tradition. 

Astrology, as an esoteric discipline, is the study of the influence of celestial bodies on human affairs and terrestrial events, suggesting that our lives are influenced by cosmic forces beyond our control. The movement of planets and stars is thought to have an impact on our individual personalities, strengths, and challenges shaping the path we walk through life. This system of divination and personal growth has been used since ancient times (e.g., Mesopotamian astrology) and has strong connections to the Hermetic maxim, "As above, so below."

Karmic cycles, generally rooted in Eastern religions such as Hinduism and Buddhism, refer to the belief in the law of cause and effect, where one's actions in a past, present, or future life determine the experiences and circumstances of subsequent lives. This concept is central to the idea of reincarnation, where a soul is reborn into different physical bodies to learn lessons and progress spiritually over multiple lifetimes.

The concept of free will, both in philosophical and esoteric contexts, is the idea that individuals have the power to make choices independent of determining factors like social pressures, genetics, or predetermined destiny. This concept is at the very core of self-determination, implying that we are the architects of our own lives, responsible for shaping our own destinies through our thoughts, actions, and decisions.

The Fates, whose origins can be traced back to ancient Greek mythology, are supernatural beings responsible for determining the course of an individual's life. They are seen as a representation of the tension between fate and free will as well as predetermination and self-determination.

Now that we have a basic understanding of the core concepts, we can analyze the interplay between astrology, karmic cycles, and free will as it pertains to The Fates. 

Astrology and karmic cycles both suggest that there are forces beyond our control that influence our lives. Astrology posits that the position of celestial bodies determines our character traits and life circumstances, while karma dictates that our past actions influence our present and future experiences. In both cases, the course of our lives is subject to predetermined forces.

However, neither astrology nor karmic cycles entirely negate the concept of free will. Astrology acknowledges that, while the stars may influence our lives, we still have the power to make choices within the framework laid out for us. Similarly, while our past actions may create the circumstances we find ourselves in, we can still make conscious choices to change our behavior and, ultimately, our future outcomes.

The Fates, too, exemplify the interplay of predetermination and self-determination. Their role in determining the length and course of one's life implies a belief in predetermination, but their very existence as three separate entities introduces an element of choice and, therefore, free will.

In conclusion, the key themes of astrology, karmic cycles, and free will all illustrate the complex relationship between fate, destiny, and individual autonomy. While the influence of The Fates, as well as celestial bodies and past actions, suggests predeterminism, these forces also leave room for the exercise of free will within the parameters they set. As such, individuals can navigate the interplay between these guiding forces and their own self-determination to create unique life paths and shape their own destinies.
