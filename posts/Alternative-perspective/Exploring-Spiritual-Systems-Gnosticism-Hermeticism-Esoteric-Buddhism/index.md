To thoroughly analyze the convergence and divergence of different branches of alternative perspectives, particularly Gnosticism, Hermeticism, and Esoteric Buddhism, in their understanding of human potential, we need to first understand the core principles of each of these systems, and then discuss the roles of symbolism, initiation, and inner transformation.

Gnosticism is based on the idea of gnosis, or knowledge, being the key to spiritual enlightenment and freedom from the material world. Gnostics believe that humans have a divine spark imprisoned in a material body and that they must undergo a process of awakening to liberate themselves from the physical realm. The ultimate goal of Gnosticism is to reunite oneself with the Pleroma, or the divine fullness of the universe.

Hermeticism is a philosophical system rooted in ancient Egypt, popularized by the teachings of Hermes Trismegistus, which espouses the idea that the material world is a reflection of the spiritual realm. At its core, Hermeticism promotes the belief in the existence of a Supreme Being and emphasizes the maxim "as above, so below," which offers the idea of unity between the macrocosm and microcosm. Hermeticism seeks to cultivate spiritual development through personal transformation, alchemy, and magical practices.

Esoteric Buddhism is a branch of Mahayana Buddhism that originated in India and later spread to Tibet, China, and Japan. It focuses on practices involving mantras, mudras, mandalas, rituals, and meditation, aiming to attain spiritual enlightenment by experiencing the true nature of reality. It emphasizes the importance of a secret knowledge that can be acquired through initiation and practices to ultimately reach enlightenment or Buddhahood.

Analyzing the Convergence and Divergence:

In terms of convergence, all three branches share a focus on self-discovery, personal transformation, and the belief in the existence of a higher, spiritual reality. Additionally, they emphasize the importance of understanding the interconnectedness between the material world and the spiritual realm.

All three systems utilize initiation as a means to access esoteric knowledge and practices, which are not available to the general public. Gnosticism uses initiation to convey secret teachings and rituals to its disciples, while Hermeticism incorporates initiation into its practice of alchemy, which aims to bring about spiritual transformation. Likewise, Esoteric Buddhism calls for its practitioners to be initiated into the secret knowledge of spiritual techniques and rituals to achieve greater spiritual progress.

Symbolism is another shared aspect of these alternative perspectives. Gnostics often use symbols to impart esoteric wisdom and explain complex cosmological concepts. Similarly, Hermeticism employs symbols heavily in its alchemical and magical practices, using them as catalysts for internal transformations. In Esoteric Buddhism, symbols like mantras, mudras, and mandalas serve as vehicles for practice, aiding practitioners in their journey to enlightenment. 

Despite these similarities, there are several divergences in their understanding of human potential. Gnosticism views the material world as corrupt and an obstacle to spiritual growth. In contrast, Hermeticism and Esoteric Buddhism recognize that understanding the relationship between the material and spiritual realms is essential for spiritual development. Additionally, Gnosticism's ultimate goal is reunification with the divine, whereas Hermeticism seeks personal transformation through alchemy and magic, and Esoteric Buddhism aims for ultimate enlightenment or Buddhahood.

In conclusion, Gnosticism, Hermeticism, and Esoteric Buddhism converge in their focus on personal transformation, initiation, spiritual development, and a higher spiritual reality. They also share a reliance on symbolism as a means to convey esoteric knowledge. However, they diverge concerning their views on the material world, the precise goals of their practices, and the means to achieve these goals. Overall, these alternative perspectives offer alternative ways to perceive and explore human potential while acknowledging the importance of spiritual growth and self-discovery.
