The Market Harborough Festival of Boats and Arts has made significant contributions to the local community over the years in several ways, including economic influence, cultural enrichment, and environmental impact. This report delves into the various aspects of these contributions by providing specific case studies and outlining the progress of the event over the past five years, culminating in projections for potential future developments.

Economic Influence:
The festival has revitalized the local economy of Market Harborough by drawing in tourists and increasing footfall in the town. Local businesses like the Market Harborough Brewery have benefited immensely from the increased number of visitors who attend the event annually. Additionally, with the continuous growth of the event and the wider reach of its promotion, the investment into local infrastructure and support services, such as catering and audiovisual equipment hire has been on the rise, directly correlating with the financial success of the festival. As a result, businesses in hospitality, retail, and entertainment sectors have seen a significant boost during the period of the festival each year.

Cultural Enrichment:
The Festival of Boats and Arts has served as a platform for local artists to showcase their work and gain recognition within the community. The event encourages participation from a diverse range of artists, bridging the gap between various art forms and promoting a sense of unity within the local creative community. An example of an artist who gained recognition through the festival is local painter, Jane Smith, whose illustrations of boats and waterfronts were displayed during the 2018 edition of the festival, resulting in increased sales and commissions for her work.

Environmental Impact:
One of the key goals of the Market Harborough Festival of Boats and Arts has been to encourage sustainable practices. The festival has worked closely with environmental organizations to ensure that its operations leave as minimal an ecological footprint as possible. For instance, the festival has encouraged eco-friendly transportation options like cycling and walking for both local attendees and visitors. Waste management practices have been put in place with effective recycling policies and initiatives to minimize the event's environmental impact.

Progress Over the Past Five Years:
The last five years have been crucial to the development of the Market Harborough Festival of Boats and Arts, with each edition showing consistent growth both in event attendance and community impact. This growth can be attributed to the successful collaboration between local businesses, artists and environmental organizations in the planning and execution of the annual event. Word-of-mouth marketing has also played a significant role in attracting more visitors to the town.

Potential Future Developments:
Based on the current trajectory, the Market Harborough Festival of Boats and Arts is projected to continue evolving into a nationally recognized event. This will lead to increased investment in local infrastructure, art development programs and environmental initiatives. It is expected that the event will grow to accommodate a larger number of visitors, therefore necessitating greater event capacity and more extensive resources. To further solidify its reputation as a flagship event in the region, the festival will need to continue promoting a diverse range of artistic representation and maintaining its commitment to environmental sustainability while seeking ways to minimize carbon footprint and maximize outreach to a wider audience.
