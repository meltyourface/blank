In order to thoroughly answer this knowledge challenge, let us first define the key terms in the question and provide a brief overview of the context within which they exist in Tantric philosophy. The microcosm and macrocosm, as well as the interplay between Shakti and Shiva energies, are essential concepts that form the foundation of Tantric thought. We must also explore the goal of Tantric practice and explain its significance in the dissolution of dualistic perception. 

Microcosm represents the individual human being, while macrocosm refers to the universe or the larger reality that the individual is a part of. In Tantric philosophy, the relationship between the microcosm and macrocosm is seen as a reflection or mirroring of each other, where the human body is considered a miniature cosmos reflecting the entirety of the universe in its structure and functions. This perspective is based on the idea that there is an underlying unity, and everything in existence is interconnected and interdependent.

The Shakti and Shiva energies represent the polarities of the cosmic existence. Shakti, often symbolized as the Divine Feminine, is the energy of creation, manifestation, and change. Shiva, on the other hand, is the masculine counterpart, representing consciousness, stillness, and pure awareness. Thus, they embody the dualistic principles of energy and matter, movement and stillness, expansion and contraction. In Tantra, these energies are seen as complementary forces that have a dynamic interplay, both within the individual and the universe as a whole.

With this foundational understanding of the concepts, let's explore how an initiate's practice in Tantra can balance the interplay between Shakti and Shiva energies, ultimately leading to the dissolution of dualistic perception. The crux of Tantric practice revolves around harnessing and directing these energies within oneself in a harmonious way. This is achieved through various tools of Tantra, including meditation, breathwork (pranayama), energy channeling, visualization, rituals, and physical exercises like yoga asanas. 

One of the primary goals of these practices is to awaken the dormant Kundalini energy at the base of the spine (the coiled serpent or Shakti energy). By doing so, the Tantric initiate endeavors to elevate this powerful energy along the central energy channel (Sushumna) until it merges with Shiva energy, the pure consciousness, in the crown chakra at the top of the head. This union of Shakti and Shiva energies symbolizes the conjoining of matter and energy, manifestation and consciousness, and brings about a transformative experience that transcends the dualistic perception of oneself and the universe.

As the Tantric practitioner progresses in their journey, they gradually come to recognize the interdependent nature of the microcosm and macrocosm, as well as the dualistic nature of Shakti and Shiva energies. Through contemplation and direct experiential understanding, they become aware that these dualities are merely different aspects of the same overarching reality. This shift in perspective allows them to transcend dualistic perception and experience the underlying unity of existence, glimpsing the non-dual nature of reality known as Advaita or Oneness.

In summary, the relationship between the microcosm and macrocosm in Tantric philosophy is one of interconnectedness and correspondence, where the individual is seen as a reflection of the universe. The balance and integration of Shakti and Shiva energies through Tantric practices enable the initiate to harness the power inherent in these polarities and unite them in a harmonious manner. This union ultimately dissolves the dualistic perception that is inherent in the action-consequence driven, human experience and paves the way for an experiential realization of the non-dual, ultimate reality.
