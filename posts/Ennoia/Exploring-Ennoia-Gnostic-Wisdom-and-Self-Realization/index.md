1. Gnosticism: Gnosticism is a collection of ancient religious ideas and systems which originated in the first century AD among early Christian sects. These systems shared a dualistic worldview, believing in a world created and ruled by a lesser divinity called the Demiurge—often associated with the God of the Old Testament—and a transcendental, unknown, supreme reality called the Pleroma, populated by divine entities called Aeons.

2. Ennoia: Ennoia, also known as Barbelo or Sophia in different Gnostic texts, is one of the highest emanations of the divine within Gnostic thought. She is often referred to as the primal thought or concept that emerged from the supreme reality in the beginning. Ennoia can be considered the cognitive aspect of the divine, representing divine thought, knowledge, and wisdom.

Divine thought is inherently connected to the supreme reality and acts as a bridge between human experience and the divine realm.
