The interconnected process and essential inner alchemical transmutations that an initiate undergoes in their transformative journey through the abyss can be better understood by examining each aspect of the Triptych and their relationship to Hermeticism, Alchemy, and Qabalah. 

1. **The Dark Night**:

The Dark Night is a period of spiritual crisis and purification, where the initiate experiences a feeling of abandonment, despair, and darkness, also known as the "nigredo" stage in alchemical terminology. This process forces the aspirant to confront and resolve their own psychological and existential issues to allow for their rebirth and a more profound understanding of their true nature. This journey can be likened to the Hermetic principle of "As above, so below," which posits that correspondence exists between all things, including the individual and the cosmos. In the context of Qabalah, the sephirah Malkuth (the physical/material world) is the starting point of this exploration, as the initiate seeks to purify the self and connect with the divine.

2. **Spiritual Alchemy**:

Following the purification of the Dark Night, the initiate is ready to engage with the transformative process of Spiritual Alchemy. This approach involves the dissolution and reintegration of the self through metaphorical and symbolic transmutations. The Hermetic tradition forms the basis for the principles employed in Spiritual Alchemy, such as the unity of opposites and the reciprocal relationship between the microcosm and macrocosm. Alchemical symbolism, such as the philosopher's stone, the union of the masculine (sulfur/sol) and feminine (mercury/luna) aspects, and the transmutation of base metals into gold, are metaphorical expressions of the transformative potential in the journey towards self-mastery.

In the framework of the Qabalah, the initiate progresses through the Tree of Life, from the lower to the higher sephiroth. This journey facilitates understanding and assimilation of the divine attributes represented by each sphere, encouraging the realization of the transcendental oneness behind all apparent distinctions.

3. **The Sacred Wedding**:

The final phase of the Triptych represents the union and integration of the various aspects of being, culminating in the marriage of the spiritual and worldly selves. This is the culmination of the process, where the practitioner becomes one with the divine. In Hermeticism, this concept is embodied by the Hermetic axiom "As above, so below," which, when fully realized, allows the individual to function as a perfected microcosm in harmony with the macrocosm.

In alchemical terminology, the Sacred Wedding is referred to as the "rubedo" or "albedo" stage, typifying the transformation of the initiate into an enlightened, integrated being. The Qabalistic counterpart is the ascent through the sephiroth until reaching Kether, the unmanifest source of creation, blending with the divine essence, and becoming an integral part of the cosmic order.

To summarize, the Triptych of the Dark Night, Spiritual Alchemy, and the Sacred Wedding symbolically represent the stages of transformation an initiate experiences in their quest for self-mastery and divine integration. By engaging with the rich and profound traditions of Hermeticism, Alchemy, and Qabalah across these phases, the aspirant gains deep insights and understanding, allowing them to transmute and integrate their being ultimately. The result is a transcendent understanding of the self and the cosmos, achieving unity with the Divine Essence and a deepened comprehension of the interconnected and interdependent nature of reality.
